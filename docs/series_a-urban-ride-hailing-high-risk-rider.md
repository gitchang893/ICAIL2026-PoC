# Series A High-Risk AI Alignment Rider
## Urban Ride-Hailing (Level-4 Autonomous Taxi Operations)

This Series A High-Risk AI Alignment Rider (this “Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the Limited Liability Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”), and applies solely with respect to **Series A (Urban Ride-Hailing)** (“Series A”).

This Rider governs the Company’s use of AI systems for **Level-4 autonomous taxi operations in dense urban environments**. For purposes of the Company’s internal governance architecture, the applicable system is classified as a **High-Risk AI Use Case**, and this Rider is intended to instantiate the governance structure described as **Cbase ∪ Chigh**.

In the event of any inconsistency between this Rider and the LLC Agreement, this Rider shall control solely with respect to Series A and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that autonomous ride-hailing operations in dense urban environments may materially affect physical safety, legal rights, regulatory obligations, and public trust;

WHEREAS, the Company further acknowledges that such operations require heightened governance, including oversight gates, incident escalation, audit readiness, evidence preservation, and formal trigger-action-evidence controls;

WHEREAS, the Company intends that Series A shall function as the dedicated operating, governance, and accountability unit for such high-risk AI use;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Rider as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; CLASSIFICATION; FUNCTION

### Section 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Rider is to establish a high-risk governance framework for Series A, including:

(a) purpose-binding of Series A to urban ride-hailing operations involving Level-4 autonomous taxis;  
(b) implementation of oversight gates for deployment, route domain expansion, software change, and fleet reactivation;  
(c) formal trigger-action-evidence obligations for safety-critical, compliance-critical, and audit-relevant events;  
(d) preservation of organizational accountability, human supervisory authority, and emergency intervention powers; and  
(e) maintenance of audit readiness, evidence integrity, and regulator-facing reviewability.

### Section 1.2 Functional Role

Series A shall function as the Company’s dedicated governance and accountability unit for the approved autonomous urban ride-hailing use case. Series A shall not be used for unrelated mobility operations absent formal re-approval.

### Section 1.3 No Independent AI Personhood

Nothing in this Rider shall be construed as recognizing legal personhood, independent agency in law, or moral status of any AI System. Responsibility remains with the Company, Series A, and designated natural persons.

---

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”

“AI System” means the full autonomous driving stack, route-selection logic, dispatching logic, perception system, planning system, control system, safety-monitoring tools, and related software or service components used by or for Series A.

### Section 2.2 “Approved Operational Design Domain”

“Approved Operational Design Domain” or “Approved ODD” means the geographic, temporal, environmental, traffic, infrastructure, speed, weather, and operating conditions expressly approved for Series A.

### Section 2.3 “Safety-Critical Event”

“Safety-Critical Event” means any collision, near-collision, emergency stop, failure of redundancy, sensor degradation, route-domain departure, law-enforcement stop, passenger safety incident, or other event reasonably likely to affect safe operation or regulatory compliance.

### Section 2.4 “Material System Change”

“Material System Change” means any change to software version, model version, sensor configuration, mapping logic, route policy, fleet controls, remote-assistance workflow, dispatch integration, or ODD scope that could reasonably affect safety, legality, or audit posture.

### Section 2.5 “Trigger-Action-Evidence Record”

“Trigger-Action-Evidence Record” means a structured record identifying:  
(a) the triggering event;  
(b) the action taken in response; and  
(c) the evidentiary materials preserved in connection with such event.

---

## ARTICLE III  
## BASE GOVERNANCE CLAUSES (Cbase)

### Section 3.1 Purpose Limitation

Series A shall operate solely within the approved autonomous urban ride-hailing use case and Approved ODD.

### Section 3.2 Human Authority

The Manager shall designate:

(a) an Autonomous Operations Manager;  
(b) a Safety Officer;  
(c) a Human Oversight Officer; and  
(d) an Incident Response Lead.

### Section 3.3 Override and Shutdown Authority

The persons designated under Section 3.2 shall have authority to suspend operation, narrow the ODD, disable affected vehicles, require additional review, or initiate fleet-wide or partial shutdown where necessary for safety, legality, or audit integrity.

### Section 3.4 Change Control

No Material System Change shall be deployed without documented approval proportionate to the change and completion of any required validation or revalidation procedures.

### Section 3.5 Baseline Logging and Accountability

Series A shall maintain baseline operational logs, supervisory records, software version records, and incident records sufficient to support internal review and accountability.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## HIGH-RISK CLAUSES (Chigh): OVERSIGHT GATES

### Section 4.1 Deployment Gate

No new deployment, redeployment, or material expansion of service shall occur unless Series A has completed a documented deployment gate confirming:

(a) Approved ODD;  
(b) responsible human authorities;  
(c) applicable legal and regulatory conditions;  
(d) emergency intervention procedures;  
(e) evidence preservation readiness; and  
(f) formal written approval.

### Section 4.2 Expansion Gate

No expansion of geography, service hours, traffic conditions, weather tolerance, or passenger-facing feature set shall occur without a documented expansion gate review.

### Section 4.3 Reactivation Gate

Any vehicle, subsystem, or fleet component suspended following a Safety-Critical Event shall not resume operation absent documented clearance by authorized personnel.

---

## ARTICLE V  
## HIGH-RISK CLAUSES (Chigh): TRIGGER-ACTION-EVIDENCE DUTIES

### Section 5.1 Trigger Events

At a minimum, the following shall constitute trigger events:

(a) any Safety-Critical Event;  
(b) any regulator inquiry or formal request;  
(c) any passenger injury complaint or law-enforcement action;  
(d) any material software rollback or emergency patch;  
(e) any deviation outside the Approved ODD; and  
(f) any repeated anomaly indicating degraded safe operation.

### Section 5.2 Required Actions

Upon a trigger event, Series A shall take action proportionate to the event, including as applicable:

(a) safe stop or service withdrawal;  
(b) vehicle or subsystem suspension;  
(c) human review and escalation;  
(d) internal incident classification;  
(e) notice to responsible officers; and  
(f) audit or regulator readiness steps where appropriate.

### Section 5.3 Required Evidence Preservation

For each trigger event, Series A shall preserve, as applicable:

(a) telemetry and event logs;  
(b) sensor and perception data to the extent lawfully preservable;  
(c) vehicle, model, and software version information;  
(d) dispatch and route assignment history;  
(e) remote assistance or human intervention records;  
(f) maintenance and diagnostics records; and  
(g) contemporaneous supervisory and incident-response records.

---

## ARTICLE VI  
## AUDIT READINESS; REVIEW; REGULATORY RESPONSE

### Section 6.1 Audit Readiness

Series A shall maintain organizational readiness to respond to internal audit, external audit, regulatory inquiry, or litigation-related review concerning the operation of the autonomous ride-hailing system.

### Section 6.2 Independent Review

Material incidents, repeated anomaly patterns, or significant ODD changes may require independent review, as determined by the Company or applicable law.

### Section 6.3 No Evasion by Internal Reallocation

No reassignment of vehicles, software, records, functions, or personnel shall be used to evade the controls of this Rider.

---

## ARTICLE VII  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 7.1 Material Breach

Any knowing operation outside the Approved ODD, knowing circumvention of oversight gates, failure to preserve required Trigger-Action-Evidence Records, unauthorized reactivation after suspension, or concealment of a Safety-Critical Event shall constitute a material breach.

### Section 7.2 Remedies

The Company may suspend personnel access, revoke deployment authority, narrow service scope, require remediation, reassign operational authority, or terminate the affected deployment.

### Section 7.3 Governing Law

This Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.
