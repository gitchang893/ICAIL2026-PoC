# Series B Ctransparency Add-On
## Executive Fleet Leasing (AI-Assisted Scheduling and Routing)

This Series B Ctransparency Add-On (this “Series B Add-On”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the LLC Agreement and expressly incorporates the **Common Cbase AI Governance Rider**. Accordingly, the governance structure applicable to Series B shall be **Cbase ∪ Ctransparency**.

This Series B Add-On applies solely with respect to **Series B (Executive Fleet Leasing)** (“Series B”), which is designated by the Company as the series responsible for **AI-assisted logistics for scheduling and routing corporate vehicles**.

In the event of any inconsistency between the Cbase Rider and this Series B Add-On, this Series B Add-On shall control solely with respect to Series B and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that AI-assisted fleet scheduling and routing may affect operational efficiency, user expectations, and contractual performance, while ordinarily not requiring the full high-risk controls applicable to Series A;

WHEREAS, the Company has classified Series B as a **limited-risk tier** use case and intends that transparency-oriented clauses shall apply in addition to the baseline governance clauses of Cbase;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Series B Add-On as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## SERIES B USE CASE; LIMITED-RISK CLASSIFICATION

### Section 1.1 Approved Use Case

The Approved Use Case Boundary for Series B shall consist solely of AI-assisted scheduling, routing, allocation, dispatch-support, and related operational support functions for executive fleet leasing operations.

### Section 1.2 Limited-Risk Classification

Series B is designated as a **limited-risk tier** AI series.

### Section 1.3 Required Personnel

In addition to any baseline personnel designated under Cbase, the Company shall designate for Series B:

(a) a Fleet Operations Manager;  
(b) a Compliance Liaison; and  
(c) a Human Supervisory Officer.

---

## ARTICLE II  
## Ctransparency CLAUSES: USER NOTICE

### Section 2.1 User Notice

Relevant internal users shall be informed that scheduling or routing outputs are AI-assisted and remain subject to human supervision and override.

### Section 2.2 No Implied Finality

Series B shall not present AI-assisted scheduling or routing outputs as self-authorizing or final where Company policy requires human operational review.

---

## ARTICLE III  
## Ctransparency CLAUSES: DOCUMENTATION

### Section 3.1 Scope Documentation

Series B shall maintain internal documentation reasonably sufficient to describe:

(a) the approved purpose of the system;  
(b) the principal functions the system performs;  
(c) the categories of data materially relied upon;  
(d) material operational limitations known to Series B; and  
(e) the roles of human reviewers and operators.

### Section 3.2 Documentation of Material Changes

Series B shall maintain internal records of Material Changes affecting the system’s scope, logic, integrations, or user-facing workflow.

### Section 3.3 Documentation of Material Workflow Dependence

Where the AI System becomes materially influential in scheduling, dispatch, or routing decisions, Series B shall ensure that responsible personnel are aware of such operational dependence and of the continued requirement for human supervision.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## DRIFT; RECLASSIFICATION

### Section 4.1 No High-Risk Drift

Series B shall not be used to conduct a high-risk or rights-affecting AI use case without formal reclassification and reassignment to the governance structure designated by the Company.

### Section 4.2 Reclassification Review

The Company shall conduct documented review if the AI System becomes materially involved in determinations affecting legal rights, legal obligations, eligibility, or other high-impact matters.

### Section 4.3 No Full High-Risk Gatekeeping by Reason of Limited-Risk Status Alone

Unless otherwise required by law or internal Company policy, Series B shall not be required under this Series B Add-On to undergo the full gatekeeping applicable to Series A solely by reason of limited-risk classification.

---

## ARTICLE V  
## BREACH; REMEDIES

### Section 5.1 Material Breach

Any knowing operation outside the Approved Use Case Boundary, knowing concealment of material workflow dependence, knowing circumvention of required user notice or supervisory authority, or knowing use of Series B for a high-risk function without reclassification shall constitute a material breach with respect to Series B.

### Section 5.2 Remedies

The Company may suspend deployment, narrow operational scope, require documentation updates, require additional supervisory review, or transfer the use case to another governance structure.
