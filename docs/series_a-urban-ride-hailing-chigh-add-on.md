# Series A Chigh Add-On
## Urban Ride-Hailing (Level-4 Autonomous Taxi Operations)

This Series A Chigh Add-On (this “Series A Add-On”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the LLC Agreement and expressly incorporates the **Common Cbase AI Governance Rider**. Accordingly, the governance structure applicable to Series A shall be **Cbase ∪ Chigh**.

This Series A Add-On applies solely with respect to **Series A (Urban Ride-Hailing)** (“Series A”), which is designated by the Company as the series responsible for **Level-4 autonomous taxi operations in dense urban environments**.

In the event of any inconsistency between the Cbase Rider and this Series A Add-On, this Series A Add-On shall control solely with respect to Series A and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that autonomous urban ride-hailing may materially affect physical safety, legal rights, regulatory obligations, public safety, and public trust;

WHEREAS, the Company has classified Series A as a **high-risk tier** use case and intends that heightened controls shall apply in addition to the baseline governance clauses of Cbase;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Series A Add-On as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## SERIES A USE CASE; HIGH-RISK CLASSIFICATION

### Section 1.1 Approved Use Case

The Approved Use Case Boundary for Series A shall consist solely of Level-4 autonomous taxi operations in dense urban environments, within the operational design domain expressly approved by the Company.

### Section 1.2 High-Risk Classification

Series A is designated as a **high-risk tier** AI series. The Company shall not remove or narrow such classification absent documented review and formal approval.

### Section 1.3 Required Personnel

In addition to any baseline personnel designated under Cbase, the Company shall designate for Series A:

(a) an Autonomous Operations Manager;  
(b) a Safety Officer;  
(c) a Human Oversight Officer; and  
(d) an Incident Response Lead.

---

## ARTICLE II  
## Chigh CLAUSES: OVERSIGHT GATES

### Section 2.1 Deployment Gate

No deployment, redeployment, or material service launch shall occur unless Series A completes a documented deployment gate confirming:

(a) approved operational design domain;  
(b) responsible human authorities;  
(c) applicable legal and regulatory conditions;  
(d) emergency intervention procedures;  
(e) evidence preservation readiness; and  
(f) formal written approval.

### Section 2.2 Expansion Gate

No expansion of geography, operating hours, traffic conditions, weather tolerance, service scope, or passenger-facing feature set shall occur without a documented expansion gate review and approval.

### Section 2.3 Reactivation Gate

Any vehicle, subsystem, or fleet component suspended following a safety-related event shall not resume operation absent documented clearance by authorized personnel.

---

## ARTICLE III  
## Chigh CLAUSES: TRIGGER-ACTION-EVIDENCE OBLIGATIONS

### Section 3.1 Trigger Events

At a minimum, the following shall constitute trigger events for Series A:

(a) collisions, near-collisions, emergency stops, or comparable safety events;  
(b) law-enforcement stops or material passenger safety complaints;  
(c) operation outside the approved operational design domain;  
(d) regulator inquiry or formal request;  
(e) emergency software rollback or patch; and  
(f) repeated anomalies indicating degraded safe operation.

### Section 3.2 Required Actions

Upon a trigger event, Series A shall take action proportionate to the event, including as applicable:

(a) safe stop or service withdrawal;  
(b) suspension of affected vehicles or subsystems;  
(c) human review and escalation;  
(d) internal incident classification; and  
(e) audit or regulator readiness steps.

### Section 3.3 Required Evidence

For each trigger event, Series A shall preserve, as applicable:

(a) telemetry and event logs;  
(b) relevant perception, mapping, or sensor records to the extent lawfully preservable;  
(c) software and model version information;  
(d) dispatch and route history;  
(e) human intervention or remote-assistance records; and  
(f) contemporaneous supervisory and incident-response records.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## AUDIT READINESS; INDEPENDENT REVIEW

### Section 4.1 Audit Readiness

Series A shall maintain organizational readiness to respond to internal audit, external audit, regulatory inquiry, or litigation-related review.

### Section 4.2 Independent Review

Material incidents, repeated anomaly patterns, or significant operational design domain changes may require independent review, as determined by the Company or applicable law.

### Section 4.3 No Evasion by Internal Reallocation

No reassignment of vehicles, software, records, functions, or personnel shall be used to evade the controls of this Series A Add-On.

---

## ARTICLE V  
## BREACH; REMEDIES

### Section 5.1 Material Breach

Any knowing operation outside the approved operational design domain, knowing circumvention of an oversight gate, failure to preserve required trigger-action-evidence records, unauthorized reactivation after suspension, or concealment of a safety-related trigger event shall constitute a material breach with respect to Series A.

### Section 5.2 Remedies

The Company may suspend personnel access, revoke deployment authority, narrow service scope, require remediation, reassign operational authority, or terminate the affected deployment.
