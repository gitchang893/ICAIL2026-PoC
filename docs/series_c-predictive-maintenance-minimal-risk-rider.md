# Series C Minimal-Risk AI Baseline Governance Rider
## Predictive Maintenance (Internal Component-Failure Prediction)

This Series C Minimal-Risk AI Baseline Governance Rider (this “Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the Limited Liability Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”), and applies solely with respect to **Series C (Predictive Maintenance)** (“Series C”).

This Rider governs the Company’s use of AI systems for **internal predictive maintenance using sensor data to anticipate component failure**. For purposes of the Company’s internal governance architecture, the applicable system is classified as a **Minimal-Risk AI Use Case**, and this Rider is intended to instantiate the governance structure described as **Cbase only**.

In the event of any inconsistency between this Rider and the LLC Agreement, this Rider shall control solely with respect to Series C and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that internal predictive maintenance tools may improve operational efficiency and maintenance planning while ordinarily imposing less external governance burden than limited-risk or high-risk AI uses;

WHEREAS, the Company nevertheless intends to preserve baseline logging, change control, accountability allocation, and incident escalation sufficient to ensure orderly governance and prevent unreviewed migration into higher-risk use;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Rider as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; CLASSIFICATION; FUNCTION

### Section 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Rider is to establish a minimal-risk governance framework for Series C, including:

(a) purpose-binding of Series C to internal predictive maintenance functions;  
(b) implementation of baseline governance controls appropriate to minimal-risk AI use;  
(c) preservation of accountability, baseline logging, and change control; and  
(d) prevention of silent migration into customer-facing, rights-affecting, or otherwise higher-risk use.

### Section 1.2 Functional Role

Series C shall function as the Company’s dedicated governance and accountability unit for the approved internal predictive maintenance use case.

### Section 1.3 No Independent AI Personhood

Nothing in this Rider shall be construed as recognizing legal personhood, independent agency in law, or moral status of any AI System. Responsibility remains with the Company, Series C, and designated natural persons.

---

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”

“AI System” means the internal model, sensor-data processing workflow, component-failure prediction logic, maintenance-priority outputs, and related software or service components used by or for Series C.

### Section 2.2 “Approved Use Case Boundary”

“Approved Use Case Boundary” means internal predictive maintenance, maintenance planning support, failure forecasting, and related internal operational functions expressly approved for Series C.

### Section 2.3 “Material Change”

“Material Change” means any change to model version, provider, sensor inputs, prediction logic, connected tools, data scope, or deployment context that could reasonably affect the legality, reliability, security, or governance posture of Series C.

### Section 2.4 “Material Incident”

“Material Incident” means any significant system failure, repeated false signal pattern, unauthorized use outside the Approved Use Case Boundary, significant data integrity failure, or other event reasonably requiring supervisory attention.

---

## ARTICLE III  
## BASE GOVERNANCE CLAUSES (Cbase)

### Section 3.1 Purpose Limitation

Series C shall operate solely within the approved internal predictive maintenance use case.

### Section 3.2 Human Authority

The Manager shall designate:

(a) a Maintenance Systems Manager; and  
(b) a Compliance or Operations Liaison.

### Section 3.3 Human Responsibility

Maintenance decisions, replacement decisions, and operational responses shall remain attributable to the Company and relevant natural persons, and shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System.

### Section 3.4 Change Control

No Material Change shall be implemented without documented review proportionate to the change.

### Section 3.5 Baseline Logging

Series C shall maintain baseline records sufficient to identify system versions, principal data sources, material workflow changes, and material incidents.

### Section 3.6 Incident Escalation

Material Incidents shall be escalated to designated responsible personnel within a reasonable time.

### Section 3.7 No Higher-Risk Drift

Series C shall not be used for customer-facing, rights-affecting, eligibility-related, or other higher-risk functions without formal reclassification and reassignment to the governance structure designated by the Company.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 4.1 Material Breach

Any knowing operation outside the Approved Use Case Boundary, knowing concealment of a Material Incident, or knowing use of Series C for a higher-risk function without reclassification shall constitute a material breach.

### Section 4.2 Remedies

The Company may suspend deployment, narrow the system’s use, require remediation, require additional review, or transfer the use case to another governance structure.

### Section 4.3 Governing Law

This Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.
