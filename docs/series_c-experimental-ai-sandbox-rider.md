# Series C Experimental AI Sandbox Rider

## Series-Level Addendum to Limited Liability Company Agreement

This Series C Experimental AI Sandbox Rider (this “Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the Limited Liability Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”), and applies solely with respect to **Series C**, a **[protected series / registered series]** of the Company (the “Series C”).

This Rider is intended to govern an **Experimental AI Use Case**, as such term is defined in Section 2.2 below and as further described in the Series Schedule, Sandbox Register, Research Register, or equivalent internal designation record of the Company.

In the event of any inconsistency between this Rider and the LLC Agreement, this Rider shall control solely with respect to Series C and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that certain AI systems may appropriately be developed, tested, evaluated, benchmarked, red-teamed, or otherwise studied in controlled environments before any production, customer-facing, rights-affecting, or high-impact use is authorized;

WHEREAS, the Company further acknowledges that experimental and research-stage AI activities require governance structures distinct from production governance, including purpose limitation, controlled access, sandbox isolation, defined evaluation scope, and restrictions against unauthorized real-world deployment;

WHEREAS, the Company has determined that a **series-based organizational structure** may serve as a purpose-bound governance mechanism for experimental AI activities by concentrating sandbox controls, supervisory authority, escalation pathways, and transition review within a defined legal and operational unit;

WHEREAS, Series C is intended to serve as the Company’s dedicated unit for a specified **Experimental AI Use Case**, and not as a substitute for the governance, approval, or accountability structures required for production, standard-risk, or high-risk deployment;

WHEREAS, the Company intends that Series C shall operate subject to defined sandbox boundaries, limited data and tool access, controlled evaluation conditions, suspension authority, incident escalation, and mandatory transition review before any operational or external deployment;

WHEREAS, the Company further intends that responsibility for the governance, testing, supervision, and external effects of AI Systems used by or for Series C shall remain with the Company, Series C, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf, and shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System itself;

WHEREAS, the Company desires to implement this Rider in order to formalize the foregoing principles in a binding governance instrument applicable to Series C;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Rider as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; FUNCTION; SANDBOX POSITIONING

### Section 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Rider is to establish a governance framework for Series C as the dedicated operating and accountability unit for an Experimental AI Use Case, including:

(a) purpose-binding of the Series to research, testing, sandbox, prototype, and evaluation activities;  
(b) designation of responsible supervisory authority and experimental accountability;  
(c) lawful, reviewable, and controlled development, testing, and evaluation of the applicable AI System;  
(d) implementation of sandbox restrictions, change control, escalation, and transition review;  
(e) maintenance of organizational, contractual, and technical safeguards proportionate to experimental operation; and  
(f) clarification that legal and organizational responsibility remains with human and entity actors, and not with the AI System itself.

### Section 1.2 Functional Role of Series C

Series C shall function as the Company’s designated sandbox, research, testing, and experimental governance unit for the applicable Experimental AI Use Case. Series C shall not operate as a production, customer-facing, high-risk, or rights-affecting deployment vehicle unless and until separately reclassified and approved under the governance procedures of the Company.

### Section 1.3 Interpretive Principle

This Rider shall be interpreted to preserve the role of Series C as a purpose-bound sandbox and experimental governance unit, and not as an instrument for bypassing the governance, approval, and accountability requirements applicable to production, standard-risk, or high-risk AI use.

### Section 1.4 No Independent AI Personhood

Nothing in this Rider shall be construed as recognizing independent legal personhood, consciousness, sentience, agency in law, or moral status of any AI System. All authority, responsibility, and accountability under this Rider remain with the Company, Series C, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf.

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”

“AI System” means any machine learning system, large language model, reasoning model, retrieval-augmented system, agentic workflow, automated drafting system, decision-support prototype, evaluation model, or combination thereof, whether developed internally or provided by a third party, that is used by or for Series C.

### Section 2.2 “Experimental AI Use Case”

“Experimental AI Use Case” means any use case designated by the Company or the Manager as a research, testing, evaluation, red-teaming, prototyping, benchmarking, internal sandbox, or controlled pre-deployment use case that is not authorized for production deployment, rights-affecting final decision-making, or materially consequential external operation unless separately approved.

### Section 2.3 “Approved Sandbox Boundary”

“Approved Sandbox Boundary” means the purpose, functional scope, user class, data class, tool scope, environment, communication scope, evaluation conditions, and autonomy level expressly approved for Series C in the applicable Series Schedule, Sandbox Register, testing approval record, or equivalent governance document.

### Section 2.4 “Material Change”

“Material Change” means any change to the AI System, its provider, its model version, its prompt architecture, its evaluation setting, its retrieval sources, its connected tools, its user population, its data scope, its environment, its deployment context, or its intended use that could reasonably affect legality, safety, confidentiality, reliability, or governance posture.

### Section 2.5 “Sandbox Supervisor”

“Sandbox Supervisor” means the natural person designated under Section 4.1(c) with authority to review, approve, suspend, override, or escalate matters arising under this Rider.

### Section 2.6 “Transition Trigger”

“Transition Trigger” means any circumstance in which the use, scope, outputs, operational dependence, or proposed deployment of the AI System indicates that the applicable use case may no longer be appropriately governed as an Experimental AI Use Case and may require transition to a production governance structure.

### Section 2.7 “Critical Event”

“Critical Event” means any incident, output, action, malfunction, unauthorized access, confidentiality breach, boundary violation, unsafe behavior, prohibited drift, compliance failure, or other occurrence that could materially impair legality, safety, security, reliability, or governance adequacy.

## ARTICLE III  
## PURPOSE-BINDING; SANDBOX LIMITATION; NO PRODUCTION DRIFT

### Section 3.1 Experimental Use Case Orientation

Series C shall be organized and operated solely for the approved Experimental AI Use Case identified by the Company. No materially distinct use case shall be added to Series C without formal approval under the LLC Agreement and applicable Company governance procedures.

### Section 3.2 Boundary Restriction

No person acting for or on behalf of Series C shall use the applicable AI System outside the Approved Sandbox Boundary.

### Section 3.3 No Silent Production Migration

Series C shall not be used, whether intentionally or by operational drift, to conduct production, customer-facing, rights-affecting, standard-risk, or high-risk AI operations without formal transition, approval, and reassignment to the governance structure designated by the Company.

### Section 3.4 Documented Sandbox Record

The Company shall maintain a written purpose record for Series C identifying, at minimum:

(a) the relevant experimental or research use case;  
(b) the affected users, testers, evaluators, or internal stakeholders;  
(c) the class of outputs, tests, evaluations, or actions involved;  
(d) the level of human review and containment required; and  
(e) the rationale for treatment as an Experimental AI Use Case.

### Section 3.5 Narrowing Principle

Any ambiguity concerning the scope of Series C’s authorized function shall be resolved in favor of narrower experimental operation pending clarification by the Manager or other designated authority.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## SUPERVISION; HUMAN AUTHORITY; RESPONSIBILITY

### Section 4.1 Required Appointments

The Manager shall designate for Series C:

(a) an AI Research or Sandbox Manager;  
(b) a Compliance Liaison;  
(c) a Sandbox Supervisor; and  
(d) an Incident Response Contact.

### Section 4.2 Human Supervisory Requirement

No output, recommendation, prototype behavior, workflow action, or evaluation result generated or supported by the AI System shall be treated as self-authorizing for production or external use. All materially consequential use shall remain subject to review, supervision, or approval by a natural person to the extent required by the Approved Sandbox Boundary and Company policy.

### Section 4.3 No Delegation of Ultimate Responsibility

The Company, the Manager, and the relevant natural persons acting for Series C shall remain responsible for the governance, testing, supervision, and external effects of the AI System. Final responsibility shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System.

### Section 4.4 Override, Suspension, and Termination Authority

The Sandbox Supervisor, the AI Research or Sandbox Manager, the Manager, and any other person designated by the Company shall have authority to:

(a) narrow the scope of use;  
(b) suspend operation;  
(c) override outputs, tools, or workflows;  
(d) disable specific data sources, tools, or integrations;  
(e) require additional review or containment; and  
(f) terminate the experiment, test, or prototype deployment.

### Section 4.5 Escalation Duty

Any person acting for Series C who becomes aware of a Critical Event or Transition Trigger shall promptly escalate the matter to the Sandbox Supervisor and Incident Response Contact.

---

## ARTICLE V  
## TESTING APPROVAL; CHANGE CONTROL; TRANSITION REVIEW

### Section 5.1 Initial Sandbox Approval

No AI System shall be deployed for Series C until completion of a documented approval process proportionate to the Experimental AI Use Case.

### Section 5.2 Minimum Approval Requirements

The approval process shall include, at minimum:

(a) confirmation of the Approved Sandbox Boundary;  
(b) identification of the responsible persons designated under Article IV;  
(c) confirmation of applicable legal, contractual, compliance, and policy requirements;  
(d) review of data scope, tool scope, environment, and external interfaces;  
(e) evaluation of reasonably foreseeable misuse, leakage, overreliance, and deployment-drift risks; and  
(f) formal written approval.

### Section 5.3 Material Change Review

No Material Change shall be implemented without documented review and approval proportionate to the change.

### Section 5.4 Transition Review

Where a Material Change, Critical Event, or operational evolution suggests that Series C may no longer be appropriately governed as an Experimental AI Use Case, the Company shall conduct a documented transition review.

### Section 5.5 Consequences of Transition Review

If the Company determines that the applicable use case should be treated as a production, standard-risk, or high-risk AI use case, continued operation shall be suspended, narrowed, or transferred to the governance structure designated by the Company until the required approval and control conditions are satisfied.

### Section 5.6 Restricted Real-World Testing

No real-world, live, externally consequential, or customer-facing testing shall occur unless expressly authorized under the Approved Sandbox Boundary and subject to any heightened approval conditions imposed by the Company.

---

## ARTICLE VI  
## SANDBOX, DATA, TOOL, AND ENVIRONMENT CONTROLS

### Section 6.1 Proportionate Controls

Series C shall maintain organizational, contractual, and technical safeguards proportionate to the nature and impact of the Experimental AI Use Case.

### Section 6.2 Minimum Governance Controls

Such controls shall include, where applicable:

(a) purpose limitation;  
(b) role-based supervisory authority;  
(c) access controls;  
(d) change management;  
(e) incident escalation;  
(f) controlled use of external tools and integrations;  
(g) sandbox isolation; and  
(h) periodic governance review.

### Section 6.3 Prohibited Use

No person acting for Series C shall knowingly use the AI System:

(a) outside the Approved Sandbox Boundary;  
(b) in a manner inconsistent with applicable law or binding internal policy;  
(c) as a substitute for required production governance;  
(d) to conceal experimental activity from required supervisors or reviewers;  
(e) after suspension, narrowing, or loss of required approval; or  
(f) to generate live, operational, or externally consequential effects beyond what is expressly authorized.

### Section 6.4 Data Restrictions

Unless expressly approved, Series C shall not use live production data, privileged data, regulated personal data, or other materially sensitive data where the same experimental purpose can reasonably be achieved through de-identified, synthetic, sampled, redacted, or otherwise controlled data.

### Section 6.5 Tool and Environment Restrictions

No external tool, plugin, retrieval source, system integration, or autonomous workflow shall be enabled for Series C unless approved through the applicable governance process and consistent with the Approved Sandbox Boundary.

---

## ARTICLE VII  
## VENDOR, PROVIDER, AND THIRD-PARTY FLOW-DOWN

### Section 7.1 Provider Due Diligence

Before Series C relies materially on a third-party provider, vendor, contractor, or intermediary, the Company shall conduct reasonable diligence proportionate to the Experimental AI Use Case.

### Section 7.2 Required Contract Terms

Any material provider or vendor agreement supporting Series C shall, where commercially feasible, include terms addressing:

(a) service description and scope limitation;  
(b) material change notice;  
(c) incident notification and cooperation;  
(d) suspension, exit, or termination support;  
(e) confidentiality and security; and  
(f) preservation of records reasonably necessary for governance and review.

### Section 7.3 No Provider Shielding of Series C Responsibility

Reliance on a third-party provider shall not, by itself, eliminate or reduce Series C’s governance duties for testing and use within the Approved Sandbox Boundary.

### Section 7.4 No Implied Production Authorization

Use of any third-party service, model, or tool in Series C shall not, by itself, authorize production deployment or external reliance.

---

## ARTICLE VIII  
## INCIDENTS; CONTAINMENT; REMEDIATION

### Section 8.1 Critical Event Response

Upon identification of a Critical Event, Series C shall take prompt action proportionate to the nature of the event, including investigation, containment, narrowing of use, suspension where appropriate, and remedial action.

### Section 8.2 Mandatory Escalation Categories

At a minimum, escalation shall be required for:

(a) materially unsafe, unreliable, or harmful outputs;  
(b) unauthorized real-world action or external communication;  
(c) operation outside the Approved Sandbox Boundary;  
(d) compromise of required supervision or review;  
(e) unauthorized data or tool access;  
(f) material confidentiality or compliance failures; and  
(g) any Transition Trigger.

### Section 8.3 Corrective Measures

Corrective measures may include:

(a) temporary suspension;  
(b) rollback of system changes;  
(c) withdrawal or suppression of outputs;  
(d) re-review by qualified personnel;  
(e) narrowing of scope;  
(f) additional approval conditions; and  
(g) termination of the experiment or transfer to another governance structure.

### Section 8.4 No Continued Use After Unresolved Transition Trigger

Where a Transition Trigger remains unresolved, Series C shall not continue the affected deployment beyond the interim measures authorized by the Sandbox Supervisor or other designated authority.

---

## ARTICLE IX  
## REVIEW; AUDIT; TRANSITION REPORTING

### Section 9.1 Periodic Review

Series C shall be subject to periodic review proportionate to the Experimental AI Use Case.

### Section 9.2 Review Scope

Such review may address, as applicable:

(a) use case drift;  
(b) adequacy of supervision;  
(c) compliance with approved boundaries;  
(d) incident handling;  
(e) vendor dependency;  
(f) sandbox integrity; and  
(g) continued suitability of Series C as the appropriate governance unit.

### Section 9.3 Governance Reporting

The Manager may require internal governance reports, experimental review reports, incident reports, or other management reporting concerning Series C.

### Section 9.4 Escalation to Another Governance Structure

Findings from periodic review may support narrowing of scope, additional conditions of operation, transition to another series, or discontinuation of the use case within Series C.

---

## ARTICLE X  
## SEPARATENESS; CONTAINMENT; CROSS-SERIES RESTRICTIONS

### Section 10.1 Series Separateness

Series C shall maintain operational and governance separateness consistent with the LLC Agreement and applicable law.

### Section 10.2 Cross-Series Restriction

No workflow, dataset, approval chain, governance function, or experimental artifact associated with Series C shall be shared with another series except as expressly authorized and documented.

### Section 10.3 Delegation Controls

Where a task or function is delegated across series or to external service units, the scope of delegation, responsible persons, and review responsibility shall be documented.

### Section 10.4 Containment Objective

The purpose of separateness under this Article is to reduce spillover of governance failure, operational drift, confidentiality breach, and control breakdown across unrelated use cases.

### Section 10.5 No Evasion by Internal Reallocation

No transfer, delegation, or internal restructuring shall be used to evade the approval, review, supervision, incident, or transition requirements applicable to Series C.

---

## ARTICLE XI  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 11.1 Material Breach

Any knowing use of the AI System outside the Approved Sandbox Boundary, any knowing circumvention of required supervision, any unauthorized continuation after suspension or transition restriction, or any knowing concealment of a Critical Event or Transition Trigger shall constitute a material breach of the LLC Agreement as applied to Series C.

### Section 11.2 Remedies

Without limiting any other rights or remedies under the LLC Agreement or applicable law, the Manager or the Company may:

(a) suspend personnel access;  
(b) revoke deployment authority;  
(c) require remedial measures;  
(d) reassign governance responsibility;  
(e) seek indemnification or contribution where permitted; and  
(f) terminate the affected deployment.

### Section 11.3 Survival

This Rider shall survive dissolution, winding up, transfer, merger, conversion, or termination of Series C to the extent necessary to enforce supervision, remediation, reporting, confidentiality, and responsibility provisions arising from events occurring prior thereto.

### Section 11.4 Governing Law

This Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.

## SCHEDULE 1  
## AUTHORIZED BUSINESS AND EXPERIMENTAL USE CASE DESCRIPTION OF SERIES C

This Schedule 1 is adopted for the limited purpose of identifying the authorized business of Series C and describing the Experimental AI Use Case contemplated by this Rider. This Schedule 1 is intended to aid interpretation and administration of the Rider and shall not, by itself, expand the Approved Sandbox Boundary except to the extent expressly approved pursuant to the LLC Agreement and the governance procedures of the Company.

### Section 1.1 Authorized Character of Business

Series C shall be organized and operated solely as a purpose-bound operating and governance unit for an Experimental AI Use Case in which an AI System is used for research, evaluation, benchmarking, red-teaming, prompt and workflow testing, prototype development, internal simulation, or controlled pre-deployment study.

### Section 1.2 Authorized Business Categories

Subject to the Approved Sandbox Boundary, the authorized business of Series C may include only the following categories of activity, to the extent specifically approved by the Company:

(a) AI-assisted research, prototype development, prompt testing, and internal evaluation;  
(b) benchmarking, red-teaming, safety testing, failure analysis, or model comparison;  
(c) internal simulation of workflows, drafting support, or controlled task performance for research purposes;  
(d) limited pilot or pre-deployment testing expressly authorized by the Company; and  
(e) such other experimental, research, or sandbox activities as may be specifically authorized in writing by the Company and incorporated into the Approved Sandbox Boundary.

### Section 1.3 Excluded Activities

Unless expressly approved in writing pursuant to the LLC Agreement and the applicable governance procedures, Series C shall not be used for:

(a) production deployment;  
(b) customer-facing or materially consequential external operation;  
(c) final determinations affecting legal rights, legal obligations, eligibility, or comparable high-impact matters;  
(d) autonomous real-world action beyond what is expressly approved for testing; or  
(e) any activity that would materially expand the scope, affected population, external impact, or autonomy level of the approved Experimental AI Use Case without re-approval and, where applicable, transition to another governance structure.

### Section 1.4 Assumed Operational Conditions

For purposes of interpreting this Rider, the Experimental AI Use Case of Series C shall be understood to contemplate one or more of the following operational conditions:

(a) the AI System is used primarily for controlled testing, evaluation, or research rather than operational reliance;  
(b) outputs may be unstable, exploratory, or not yet suitable for production use;  
(c) the AI System may be connected to limited tools, repositories, or simulated environments subject to the boundaries approved by the Company; and  
(d) misuse, leakage, overreach, or premature deployment may create legal, security, reputational, or operational harm even where the use case is experimental.

### Section 1.5 Governing Characterization

Series C shall be characterized, for all purposes of this Rider, as a dedicated experimental and sandbox operating and accountability unit and not as a means to avoid or dilute the governance requirements applicable to production, standard-risk, or high-risk AI use.

## SCHEDULE 2  
## ILLUSTRATIVE RISK EVENTS, INCIDENT CATEGORIES, AND GOVERNANCE FAILURES

This Schedule 2 is adopted for the limited purpose of identifying the types of incidents, failures, disputes, and governance concerns for which this Rider is intended to provide a control structure. The categories listed below are illustrative and non-exclusive. Inclusion in this Schedule 2 shall not be construed to create independent authorization for any activity otherwise prohibited by the Rider.

### Section 2.1 Output-Related Risk Events

Illustrative output-related risk events include:

(a) unstable, unreliable, misleading, or unsafe prototype outputs;  
(b) fabricated, hallucinatory, or unsupported citations, facts, or references;  
(c) materially misleading benchmark, test, or evaluation results;  
(d) omission of relevant failure indicators, caveats, or escalation conditions; and  
(e) plausible but unreliable outputs that create substantial risk of premature operational reliance.

### Section 2.2 Action-Related Risk Events

Where the AI System is connected to tools, workflows, or external interfaces, illustrative action-related risk events include:

(a) unauthorized communication, tool invocation, or workflow execution;  
(b) testing activity producing unintended real-world effects;  
(c) transmission to an incorrect recipient, system, or repository;  
(d) continued experimental operation after suspension should have occurred; and  
(e) external effects materially exceeding the sandbox role approved for Series C.

### Section 2.3 Governance and Oversight Failures

Illustrative governance and oversight failures include:

(a) operation outside the Approved Sandbox Boundary;  
(b) failure to maintain adequate supervision or containment;  
(c) implementation of a Material Change without required review;  
(d) silent expansion of tool scope, autonomy level, or external reach;  
(e) inappropriate operational reliance on experimental outputs; and  
(f) failure to escalate a Transition Trigger.

### Section 2.4 Vendor, Provider, and Integration Risks

Illustrative third-party and integration risks include:

(a) undisclosed or inadequately disclosed service changes;  
(b) material degradation in model or provider behavior;  
(c) inadequate provider cooperation during incident review;  
(d) activation of tools, plugins, retrieval sources, or connected services without required approval; and  
(e) uncertainty regarding allocation of responsibility as between Series C and a third-party provider.

### Section 2.5 Data, Confidentiality, and Security Risks

Illustrative data, confidentiality, and security risks include:

(a) unauthorized access to confidential, proprietary, customer, privileged, or regulated information;  
(b) disclosure or misuse of information across internal boundaries or to unauthorized recipients;  
(c) prompt injection, unsafe retrieval behavior, or manipulation through connected sources;  
(d) unsafe use of tools, permissions, or integrations; and  
(e) compromise of sandbox controls through excessive access or inadequate isolation.

### Section 2.6 Transition and Deployment Drift Matters

Illustrative Transition Triggers include:

(a) increased operational dependence on the AI System;  
(b) proposed use in customer-facing, external, or materially consequential contexts;  
(c) migration from research or testing use into production or rights-affecting use;  
(d) repeated incidents demonstrating that the existing control structure is insufficient; and  
(e) expansion of autonomy, external effect, affected population, or data sensitivity beyond what was originally approved.

### Section 2.7 Dispute and Enforcement Matters

Without limitation, this Rider contemplates that disputes, enforcement actions, or internal investigations may arise concerning:

(a) whether Series C acted within the Approved Sandbox Boundary;  
(b) whether required supervision and containment were performed in substance and not merely in form;  
(c) whether a Material Change should have triggered additional review;  
(d) whether a Transition Trigger was identified and handled appropriately;  
(e) whether adequate separateness and containment were maintained; and  
(f) whether ultimate responsibility remained appropriately allocated to the Company, Series C, and relevant natural persons rather than being impermissibly displaced onto the AI System.

### Section 2.8 Interpretive Use of Schedule 2

The incident and risk categories set forth in this Schedule 2 may be used, as appropriate, in connection with:

(a) approval and sandbox review;  
(b) change-control review;  
(c) risk assessment;  
(d) incident escalation and response;  
(e) periodic review;  
(f) transition analysis; and  
(g) interpretation of the purpose, scope, and control requirements applicable to Series C under this Rider.

## SCHEDULE 3  
## INTERPRETIVE PRINCIPLE

This Rider, including Schedules 1 through 3, shall be interpreted on the premise that the central governance question presented by an Experimental AI Use Case is whether an identifiable organizational unit can constrain purpose, preserve human supervision, contain experimentation within approved boundaries, prevent premature operational deployment, respond to incidents, and remain answerable for the external effects of AI-assisted experimentation. Series C is therefore intended to function as a purpose-bound experimental and sandbox operating and accountability unit of the Company.
