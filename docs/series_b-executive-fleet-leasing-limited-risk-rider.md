# Series B Limited-Risk AI Transparency Rider
## Executive Fleet Leasing (AI-Assisted Scheduling and Routing)

This Series B Limited-Risk AI Transparency Rider (this “Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the Limited Liability Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”), and applies solely with respect to **Series B (Executive Fleet Leasing)** (“Series B”).

This Rider governs the Company’s use of AI systems for **AI-assisted scheduling and routing of corporate vehicles in executive fleet leasing operations**. For purposes of the Company’s internal governance architecture, the applicable system is classified as a **Limited-Risk AI Use Case**, and this Rider is intended to instantiate the governance structure described as **Cbase ∪ Ctransparency**.

In the event of any inconsistency between this Rider and the LLC Agreement, this Rider shall control solely with respect to Series B and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that AI-assisted fleet scheduling and routing may affect operational efficiency, customer treatment, internal prioritization, and contractual performance, while ordinarily not requiring the full conformity-style governance applicable to high-risk uses;

WHEREAS, the Company intends that such use shall remain subject to purpose limitation, documented supervision, transparency to relevant users, and appropriate documentation of system scope and limitations;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Rider as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; CLASSIFICATION; FUNCTION

### Section 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Rider is to establish a limited-risk governance framework for Series B, including:

(a) purpose-binding of Series B to executive fleet scheduling and routing functions;  
(b) implementation of baseline governance controls applicable to limited-risk AI use;  
(c) provision of transparency, user notice, and operational documentation concerning AI-assisted scheduling and routing;  
(d) preservation of human supervisory authority and override capability; and  
(e) prevention of silent migration into high-risk or rights-affecting use.

### Section 1.2 Functional Role

Series B shall function as the Company’s dedicated governance and accountability unit for the approved executive fleet leasing use case.

### Section 1.3 No Independent AI Personhood

Nothing in this Rider shall be construed as recognizing legal personhood, independent agency in law, or moral status of any AI System. Responsibility remains with the Company, Series B, and designated natural persons.

---

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”

“AI System” means the scheduling, routing, allocation, dispatch-assistance, prioritization, optimization, and related software or service components used by or for Series B.

### Section 2.2 “Approved Use Case Boundary”

“Approved Use Case Boundary” means the scheduling, routing, dispatch-support, vehicle-allocation, and related operational support functions expressly approved for Series B.

### Section 2.3 “Material Change”

“Material Change” means any change to model version, provider, scheduling logic, routing logic, data inputs, optimization rules, connected tools, or user-facing workflow that could reasonably affect legality, transparency, fairness, operational suitability, or governance posture.

### Section 2.4 “Relevant User”

“Relevant User” means any dispatcher, fleet operator, employee, contractor, or other person whose work materially relies on AI-assisted scheduling or routing outputs.

---

## ARTICLE III  
## BASE GOVERNANCE CLAUSES (Cbase)

### Section 3.1 Purpose Limitation

Series B shall operate solely within the approved executive fleet scheduling and routing use case.

### Section 3.2 Human Authority

The Manager shall designate:

(a) a Fleet Operations Manager;  
(b) a Compliance Liaison; and  
(c) a Human Supervisory Officer.

### Section 3.3 Override Authority

Authorized personnel shall retain authority to override, revise, suspend, or reject AI-assisted scheduling, routing, or allocation outputs.

### Section 3.4 Change Control

No Material Change shall be implemented without documented review proportionate to the change.

### Section 3.5 Baseline Logging and Accountability

Series B shall maintain baseline records sufficient to identify the system version in use, relevant personnel, major workflow changes, and material incidents or complaints.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## TRANSPARENCY CLAUSES (Ctransparency)

### Section 4.1 User Notice

Relevant Users shall be informed that scheduling or routing outputs are AI-assisted and are subject to human supervision and override.

### Section 4.2 Documentation of Scope and Limits

Series B shall maintain internal documentation reasonably sufficient to describe:

(a) the approved purpose of the system;  
(b) the principal functions the system performs;  
(c) the categories of data materially relied upon;  
(d) the principal operational limitations known to Series B; and  
(e) the roles of human reviewers and operators.

### Section 4.3 Documentation of Material Changes

Series B shall maintain internal records of Material Changes affecting the system’s scope, logic, integrations, or user-facing operation.

### Section 4.4 Notice of Material Workflow Dependence

Where the AI System becomes materially influential in scheduling, dispatch, or routing decisions, Series B shall ensure that responsible personnel are aware of such operational dependence and of the continued requirement for human supervision.

### Section 4.5 No Full Conformity-Style Assessment Required

Unless otherwise required by law or internal Company policy, Series B shall not be required under this Rider to undergo the full conformity-style gatekeeping applicable to Series A solely by reason of limited-risk classification.

---

## ARTICLE V  
## DRIFT; RECLASSIFICATION; INCIDENTS

### Section 5.1 No High-Risk Drift

Series B shall not be used to conduct a high-risk or rights-affecting AI use case without formal reclassification and reassignment to the governance structure designated by the Company.

### Section 5.2 Reclassification Trigger

The Company shall conduct documented review if the AI System becomes materially involved in determinations affecting legal rights, legal obligations, eligibility, or other high-impact matters.

### Section 5.3 Incident Escalation

Material scheduling failures, routing anomalies, repeated customer-impact incidents, unauthorized automation, or materially misleading outputs shall be escalated to the designated supervisory personnel.

---

## ARTICLE VI  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 6.1 Material Breach

Any knowing operation outside the Approved Use Case Boundary, knowing concealment of material workflow dependence, knowing circumvention of required user notice or supervisory authority, or knowing use of Series B for a high-risk function without reclassification shall constitute a material breach.

### Section 6.2 Remedies

The Company may suspend deployment, narrow operational scope, require documentation updates, require additional supervisory review, or transfer the use case to another governance structure.

### Section 6.3 Governing Law

This Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.
