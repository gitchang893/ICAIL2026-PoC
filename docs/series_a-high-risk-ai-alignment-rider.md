# Series A High-Risk AI Alignment Rider (Recital Version)

## Series-Level Addendum to Limited Liability Company Agreement

This Series A High-Risk AI Alignment Rider (this “Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the Limited Liability Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”), and applies solely with respect to **Series A**, a **[protected series / registered series]** of the Company (the “Series A”).

This Rider is intended to govern a **High-Risk AI Use Case**, as such term is defined in Section 2.2 below and as further described in the Series Schedule, Use Case Register, or equivalent internal designation record of the Company.

In the event of any inconsistency between this Rider and the LLC Agreement, this Rider shall control solely with respect to Series A and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that certain AI-assisted and agentic systems may be used in contexts in which their outputs, recommendations, actions, or failures may materially affect legal rights, legal obligations, compliance posture, financial interests, safety, security, or other substantial interests;

WHEREAS, the Company further acknowledges that the ethical and legal governance of such systems cannot be reduced to a demand for fuller internal explainability alone, but also requires an identifiable institutional unit capable of bearing oversight duties, preserving reviewability, constraining operational purpose, and allocating responsibility;

WHEREAS, the Company has determined that a **series-based organizational structure** may serve as a purpose-bound governance mechanism for specified AI use cases by concentrating oversight, change control, escalation pathways, and accountability at a defined legal and operational node;

WHEREAS, Series A is intended to serve as the Company’s dedicated unit for a specified **High-Risk AI Use Case**, and not as a general-purpose AI experimentation vehicle or a repository for unrelated deployments;

WHEREAS, the Company intends that Series A shall operate subject to defined use-case boundaries, designated human oversight, deployment gating, change management, suspension and shutdown authority, incident escalation, and such other governance controls as are appropriate to high-risk operation;

WHEREAS, the Company further intends that responsibility for the governance, deployment, supervision, and external effects of AI Systems used by or for Series A shall remain with the Company, Series A, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf, and shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System itself;

WHEREAS, the Company desires to implement this Rider in order to formalize the foregoing principles in a binding governance instrument applicable to Series A;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Rider as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; REGULATORY POSTURE; FUNCTION

### Section 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Rider is to establish a governance framework for Series A as the dedicated operating and accountability unit for a High-Risk AI Use Case, including:

(a) purpose-binding of the Series to a defined high-risk use case;  
(b) designation of responsible human oversight and ultimate authority;  
(c) lawful, safe, and reviewable development, deployment, and operation of the applicable AI System;  
(d) implementation of approval gates, suspension authority, and controlled change management;  
(e) maintenance of organizational, contractual, and technical safeguards proportionate to high-risk operation; and  
(f) clarification that legal and organizational responsibility remains with human and entity actors, and not with the AI System itself.

### Section 1.2 Functional Role of Series A

Series A shall function as the Company’s designated governance, deployment, and accountability unit for the applicable High-Risk AI Use Case. Series A shall not operate as a general-purpose vehicle for unrelated AI activities.

### Section 1.3 Interpretive Principle

This Rider shall be interpreted to preserve the role of Series A as a purpose-bound governance and accountability unit for a High-Risk AI Use Case, and not as an instrument for diluting, evading, or displacing human or entity responsibility.

### Section 1.4 No Independent AI Personhood

Nothing in this Rider shall be construed as recognizing independent legal personhood, consciousness, sentience, agency in law, or moral status of any AI System. All authority, responsibility, and accountability under this Rider remain with the Company, Series A, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf.

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”

“AI System” means any machine learning system, large language model, reasoning model, retrieval-augmented system, agentic workflow, automated decision-support system, or combination thereof, whether developed internally or provided by a third party, that is used by or for Series A.

### Section 2.2 “High-Risk AI Use Case”

“High-Risk AI Use Case” means any use case designated by the Company or the Manager as requiring heightened governance because the relevant AI System may materially affect:

(a) legal rights or legal obligations;  
(b) access to important services, opportunities, or benefits;  
(c) regulatory compliance, reporting, or filing positions;  
(d) financial status, payment decisions, or eligibility determinations;  
(e) contractual commitments or dispute positions;  
(f) health, safety, security, or other substantial interests of individuals or organizations; or  
(g) any other matter designated by the Company as requiring high-risk treatment.

### Section 2.3 “Approved Use Case Boundary”

“Approved Use Case Boundary” means the purpose, functional scope, user class, data class, tool scope, decision scope, and autonomy level expressly approved for Series A in the applicable Series Schedule, Use Case Register, deployment approval record, or equivalent governance document.

### Section 2.4 “Material Change”

“Material Change” means any change to the AI System, its provider, its model version, its prompt architecture, its retrieval sources, its connected tools, its degree of autonomy, its human-review threshold, its data scope, its deployment context, or its intended use that could reasonably affect legal, safety, compliance, or governance posture.

### Section 2.5 “Human Oversight Officer”

“Human Oversight Officer” means the natural person designated under Section 4.1(c) with authority to review, approve, suspend, override, or escalate matters arising under this Rider.

### Section 2.6 “Deployment Gate”

“Deployment Gate” means the formal approval checkpoint required before initial deployment, material redeployment, or expanded operational use of the applicable AI System by Series A.

### Section 2.7 “Critical Event”

“Critical Event” means any incident, output, action, recommendation, malfunction, breach, unauthorized access, unsafe behavior, prohibited drift, compliance failure, or other event that could materially impair legality, safety, fairness, reliability, security, or accountability.

### Section 2.8 “Independent Review”

“Independent Review” means a review, audit, assessment, or governance evaluation conducted by persons or bodies not directly responsible for the day-to-day operation of the AI System at issue.

## ARTICLE III  
## PURPOSE-BINDING; USE-CASE LIMITATION

### Section 3.1 Single High-Risk Use Case Orientation

Series A shall be organized and operated solely for the approved High-Risk AI Use Case identified by the Company. No materially distinct AI use case shall be added to Series A without formal approval under the LLC Agreement and applicable Company governance procedures.

### Section 3.2 Boundary Restriction

No person acting for or on behalf of Series A shall use the applicable AI System outside the Approved Use Case Boundary.

### Section 3.3 No General-Purpose Expansion

Series A shall not be used as a general-purpose AI operations vehicle, catch-all experimental unit, or repository for multiple unrelated deployments absent formal amendment and re-approval.

### Section 3.4 Documented Purpose Record

The Company shall maintain a written purpose record for Series A identifying, at minimum:

(a) the relevant use case;  
(b) the population or counterparties potentially affected;  
(c) the class of decisions, recommendations, or outputs involved;  
(d) the required human-review threshold; and  
(e) the legal, compliance, or safety rationale for high-risk treatment.

### Section 3.5 Purpose Integrity

Any ambiguity concerning the scope of Series A’s authorized function shall be resolved in favor of narrower operation pending clarification by the Manager or other designated authority.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## HUMAN AUTHORITY; OVERSIGHT; NON-DELEGATION OF FINAL RESPONSIBILITY

### Section 4.1 Required Appointments

The Manager shall designate for Series A:

(a) an AI Compliance Manager;  
(b) a Risk Control Officer;  
(c) a Human Oversight Officer; and  
(d) an Incident Response Lead.

### Section 4.2 Human Approval Requirement

No output, recommendation, classification, decision support result, or agentic action generated or supported by the AI System shall be treated as final where it may materially affect legal rights, legal obligations, eligibility, filings, payments, contractual commitments, compliance posture, or significant external effects unless reviewed and approved by an authorized natural person.

### Section 4.3 No Delegation of Ultimate Responsibility

The Company, the Manager, and the relevant natural persons acting for Series A shall remain responsible for the governance, deployment, use, supervision, and external effects of the AI System. Final responsibility shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System.

### Section 4.4 Override, Veto, and Shutdown Authority

The Human Oversight Officer, the AI Compliance Manager, the Manager, and any other person designated by the Company shall have authority to:

(a) halt deployment;  
(b) suspend operation;  
(c) override outputs or decisions;  
(d) disable specific tools or integrations;  
(e) require additional review; and  
(f) initiate emergency shutdown procedures.

### Section 4.5 Escalation Duty

Any person acting for Series A who becomes aware of a Critical Event shall promptly escalate the matter to the Human Oversight Officer and Incident Response Lead.

### Section 4.6 Reviewability Principle

Series A shall be operated in a manner reasonably designed to permit review of material decisions, actions, overrides, and escalations by authorized human actors.

---

## ARTICLE V  
## DEPLOYMENT GATE; CHANGE CONTROL; REVALIDATION

### Section 5.1 Initial Deployment Gate

No AI System shall be deployed for Series A until completion of a documented Deployment Gate process.

### Section 5.2 Minimum Deployment Gate Requirements

The Deployment Gate shall include, at minimum:

(a) confirmation of the Approved Use Case Boundary;  
(b) identification of the responsible persons designated under Article IV;  
(c) confirmation of applicable legal and compliance requirements;  
(d) review of data scope, tool scope, and external interfaces;  
(e) evaluation of reasonably foreseeable misuse, failure, and spillover risks;  
(f) confirmation of required human review points; and  
(g) formal written approval.

### Section 5.3 Material Change Review

No Material Change shall be implemented without documented review and approval proportionate to the change.

### Section 5.4 Revalidation

Where a Material Change could affect safety, legality, reliability, external impact, or governance posture, Series A shall complete revalidation before continued or expanded use.

### Section 5.5 Restricted Experimental Use

Experimental, sandboxed, or exploratory use involving Series A’s High-Risk AI Use Case shall remain subject to this Rider and shall not be used to avoid deployment controls.

### Section 5.6 No Silent Expansion

No expansion in operational scope, affected population, decision significance, or degree of autonomy shall occur without documented approval under this Article V.

---

## ARTICLE VI  
## SAFETY, COMPLIANCE, AND OPERATIONAL CONTROLS

### Section 6.1 Proportionate Controls

Series A shall maintain organizational, contractual, and technical safeguards proportionate to the nature and impact of the High-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 6.2 Minimum Governance Controls

Such controls shall include, where applicable:

(a) purpose limitation;  
(b) role-based approval authority;  
(c) separation of duties for approval and incident response;  
(d) access controls;  
(e) change management;  
(f) incident escalation;  
(g) controlled use of external tools and integrations; and  
(h) periodic governance review.

### Section 6.3 Prohibited Use

No person acting for Series A shall knowingly use the AI System:

(a) outside the Approved Use Case Boundary;  
(b) in a manner inconsistent with applicable law or binding internal policy;  
(c) without required human approval;  
(d) to conceal automated action from required reviewers; or  
(e) after suspension, shutdown, or loss of required approval.

### Section 6.4 Outputs Not Self-Authorizing

No output of the AI System shall, by itself, constitute sufficient authorization for final action in a High-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 6.5 Alignment with Series Purpose

All use of the AI System by or for Series A shall be interpreted and controlled in light of the approved purpose of Series A and the Company’s stated governance objectives for the relevant High-Risk AI Use Case.

---

## ARTICLE VII  
## VENDOR, PROVIDER, AND THIRD-PARTY FLOW-DOWN

### Section 7.1 Provider Due Diligence

Before Series A relies materially on a third-party provider, vendor, contractor, or intermediary, the Company shall conduct reasonable diligence proportionate to the High-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 7.2 Required Contract Terms

Any material provider or vendor agreement supporting Series A shall, where commercially feasible, include terms addressing:

(a) service description and scope limitation;  
(b) material change notice;  
(c) incident notification and cooperation;  
(d) compliance support;  
(e) audit or review cooperation, where appropriate;  
(f) suspension, exit, or termination support;  
(g) confidentiality and security; and  
(h) preservation of records reasonably necessary for governance and review.

### Section 7.3 No Provider Shielding of Series A Responsibility

Reliance on a third-party provider shall not, by itself, eliminate or reduce Series A’s governance duties for deployment and use within the Approved Use Case Boundary.

### Section 7.4 Tool and Integration Control

No external tool, plugin, retrieval source, or connected service shall be enabled for Series A unless approved through the applicable governance process.

### Section 7.5 Substitute Review Materials

Where provider limitations restrict direct inspection of internal model processes, Series A shall obtain and preserve such substitute governance materials as are reasonably available, including service descriptions, change notices, audit artifacts, evaluation summaries, or provider assurances.

---

## ARTICLE VIII  
## INCIDENTS; HARM PREVENTION; REMEDIATION

### Section 8.1 Critical Event Response

Upon identification of a Critical Event, Series A shall take prompt action proportionate to the nature of the event, including investigation, containment, suspension, user notice where required, and remedial action.

### Section 8.2 Mandatory Escalation Categories

At a minimum, escalation shall be required for:

(a) materially unsafe or unlawful outputs;  
(b) unauthorized autonomous action;  
(c) outputs or actions with significant external impact;  
(d) compromise of required human review;  
(e) unauthorized data or tool access;  
(f) material compliance failures; and  
(g) repeated or systemic reliability failures.

### Section 8.3 Corrective Measures

Corrective measures may include:

(a) temporary suspension;  
(b) rollback of system changes;  
(c) withdrawal of outputs;  
(d) re-review by qualified personnel;  
(e) narrowing of scope;  
(f) additional approval conditions; and  
(g) permanent discontinuation of the deployment.

### Section 8.4 No Resumption Without Clearance

Where a Critical Event results in suspension, Series A shall not resume the affected deployment unless authorized by the Human Oversight Officer or other designated authority after documented review.

### Section 8.5 Harm-Minimization Principle

Where uncertainty exists regarding the legality, safety, reliability, or governance adequacy of continued operation, Series A shall favor temporary restriction, escalation, or pause over continued unreviewed operation.

---

## ARTICLE IX  
## INDEPENDENT REVIEW; AUDIT; GOVERNANCE REPORTING

### Section 9.1 Periodic Review

Series A shall be subject to periodic review proportionate to the High-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 9.2 Independent Review

Where designated by the Company, by applicable internal governance rules, or by law, Series A shall undergo independent review, audit, or assessment of its governance, controls, and operating posture.

### Section 9.3 Scope of Review

Such review may address, as applicable:

(a) use case drift;  
(b) adequacy of human oversight;  
(c) compliance with approved boundaries;  
(d) incident handling;  
(e) vendor dependency;  
(f) deployment controls; and  
(g) continued suitability of Series A as the appropriate governance unit.

### Section 9.4 Governance Reporting

The Manager may require internal governance reports, risk reports, incident reports, or board-level reporting concerning Series A.

### Section 9.5 Corrective Governance Action

Findings from Independent Review may support narrowing of operational scope, redesign of oversight pathways, additional conditions of deployment, reassignment of authority, or discontinuation of the use case within Series A.

---

## ARTICLE X  
## SEPARATENESS; RISK SEGREGATION; CROSS-SERIES RESTRICTIONS

### Section 10.1 Series Separateness

Series A shall maintain operational and governance separateness consistent with the LLC Agreement and applicable law.

### Section 10.2 Cross-Series Restriction

No materially sensitive workflow, dataset, approval chain, or governance function associated with Series A’s High-Risk AI Use Case shall be shared with another series except as expressly authorized and documented.

### Section 10.3 Delegation Controls

Where a task or function is delegated across series or to external service units, the scope of delegation, responsible persons, and review responsibility shall be documented.

### Section 10.4 Containment Objective

The purpose of separateness under this Article is to reduce spillover of governance failure, legal exposure, unsafe operational practice, and control breakdown across unrelated use cases.

### Section 10.5 No Evasion by Internal Reallocation

No transfer, delegation, or internal restructuring shall be used to evade the approval, review, incident, or accountability requirements applicable to Series A.

---

## ARTICLE XI  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 11.1 Material Breach

Any knowing use of the AI System outside the Approved Use Case Boundary, any knowing circumvention of required human review, any unauthorized reactivation after suspension, or any knowing concealment of a Critical Event shall constitute a material breach of the LLC Agreement as applied to Series A.

### Section 11.2 Remedies

Without limiting any other rights or remedies under the LLC Agreement or applicable law, the Manager or the Company may:

(a) suspend personnel access;  
(b) revoke deployment authority;  
(c) require remedial measures;  
(d) reassign governance responsibility;  
(e) seek indemnification or contribution where permitted; and  
(f) terminate the affected deployment.

### Section 11.3 Survival

This Rider shall survive dissolution, winding up, transfer, merger, conversion, or termination of Series A to the extent necessary to enforce oversight, remediation, reporting, confidentiality, and responsibility provisions arising from events occurring prior thereto.

### Section 11.4 Governing Law

This Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.
