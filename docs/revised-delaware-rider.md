# Delaware High-Risk AI Alignment Rider

## Trace Governance, Evidentiary Preservation, and Purpose-Binding Rider
**(Series-Level Addendum to Limited Liability Company Agreement)**

This Trace Governance, Evidentiary Preservation, and Purpose-Binding Rider
(this “Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the Limited Liability
Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, a Delaware limited liability company
(the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”), and applies solely
with respect to **[NAME OF SERIES]**, a **[protected series / registered series]** of the
Company (the “Series”).

In the event of any inconsistency between this Rider and the LLC Agreement, this
Rider shall control solely with respect to the Series and solely to the extent of such
inconsistency.

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; FUNCTION

### Section 1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Rider is to establish a series-level governance framework for
AI-assisted and agentic operations conducted by or for the Series, including:

(a) preservation of auditable Trace Records;  
(b) concentration of accountability at an identifiable legal node;  
(c) separation of action-record accountability from reasoning-expression review;  
(d) control of access to sensitive reasoning-related information;  
(e) mitigation of extraction, distillation, reverse-engineering, and spillover risks; and  
(f) implementation of purpose-binding, human oversight, and reviewability.

### Section 1.2 Functional Role of the Series
The Series shall function as the trace-managing and accountability-bearing entity for
the applicable AI use case, and shall hold, manage, preserve, and govern the Trace
Records associated with that use case.

### Section 1.3 No Ontological Commitment
Nothing in this Rider shall be construed as recognizing consciousness, sentience,
moral status, or independent legal personhood of any AI System. This Rider is
purely a governance and accountability instrument.

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”
“AI System” means any large language model, reasoning model,
retrieval-augmented system, workflow agent, multi-agent system, automated
decision-support system, or combination thereof, whether hosted internally or by a
third party, that is used by or for the Series.

### Section 2.2 “Trace Records”
“Trace Records” means the records reasonably necessary to reconstruct,
review, test, audit, and preserve the relevant operational history of a material AI-
assisted event, output, decision, recommendation, or action. Trace Records include,
as applicable:

(a) **“Reasoning Trace”**: externally expressed reasoning representations, including
chain-of-thought-like reasoning expressions, intermediate rationale fields,
deliberative text, or explanation artifacts, to the extent surfaced, logged, or
contractually available;

(b) **“Action Trace”**: records of external tool calls, API invocations, code execution,
state transitions, workflow actions, submissions, transmissions, payments,
updates, deletions, escalations, and other executed or proposed operational acts;

(c) **“Observation Trace”**: records of retrieved information, search results, database
returns, environmental observations, tool outputs, model outputs from connected
systems, sensor or multimodal inputs, and other observed external inputs;

(d) prompts, instructions, templates, policies, guardrails, and workflow settings in
effect at the relevant time;

(e) model identity, provider, version, material configuration, and relevant deployment
settings;

(f) timestamps, provenance metadata, access logs, cryptographic hashes, signature
records, and chain-of-custody data;

(g) human review, override, approval, rejection, escalation, and sign-off records; and

(h) incident, anomaly, audit, remediation, complaint, or red-team records relating to
the applicable use case.

### Section 2.3 “Covered System”
“Covered System” means any AI System used by the Series in a use case
that may materially affect legal rights, legal obligations, financial interests,
regulatory posture, external communications, filing positions, contractual activity,
or other high-impact operational outcomes.

### Section 2.4 “Covered Event”
“Covered Event” means any material output, recommendation,
classification, submission, transaction, workflow action, or external representation
generated, assisted, selected, or executed by a Covered System.

### Section 2.5 “Reasoning Trace Limitation”
“Reasoning Trace” is an externally expressed reasoning representation and not, absent
specific proof to the contrary, a complete or faithful record of hidden internal model
reasoning.

### Section 2.6 “Legal Hold Event”
“Legal Hold Event” means any threatened or actual claim, litigation,
arbitration, investigation, regulator inquiry, subpoena, audit, preservation demand,
material incident, internal complaint, or circumstance in which a reasonable person
would conclude that evidence preservation is advisable.

## ARTICLE III  
## ACCOUNTABILITY ARCHITECTURE

### Section 3.1 Accountability Concentration
For each Covered System operated for the Series, the Series shall serve as the
primary contractual and operational accountability node for the applicable use case,
subject to the LLC Agreement and any applicable upstream liability of the Company,
the Manager, or other responsible Persons.

### Section 3.2 Action Trace Primacy
For purposes of audit, reconstruction, attribution, and accountability, Action Trace
shall be the primary evidentiary record of what the Series or its Covered System did
or attempted to do.

### Section 3.3 Limited Role of Reasoning Trace
Reasoning Trace may be used as explanatory, diagnostic, safety, or audit-supporting
material, but shall not, standing alone, constitute the sole or conclusive basis for
assigning legal responsibility, fault, or breach.

### Section 3.4 Design and Oversight Responsibility
Nothing in this Rider eliminates or limits the distinct responsibilities of model
providers, system designers, deployers, managers, reviewers, or users for design,
configuration, supervision, monitoring, or final decision-making.

## ARTICLE IV  
## GOVERNANCE; HUMAN OVERSIGHT; PURPOSE-BINDING

### Section 4.1 Appointments
The Manager shall designate:

(a) an AI Compliance Manager;  
(b) an Evidence Custodian; and  
(c) a Human Oversight Officer for the Series.

### Section 4.2 Human Review
No Covered Event that may materially affect legal rights, contractual obligations,
regulatory submissions, dispute positions, or external representations shall be used
as final without review and approval by a natural person possessing authority and
competence appropriate to the matter.

### Section 4.3 Override and Shutdown Authority
The Company and/or the Manager shall maintain the right to suspend, restrict,
override, or terminate any Covered System or Covered Event where necessary for
legal compliance, safety, security, evidentiary preservation, or purpose integrity.

### Section 4.4 Purpose-Binding
The Series shall operate only within the purpose, risk class, and business function
assigned to it under the LLC Agreement and the Series schedule. No material
expansion of purpose, tool scope, data scope, or autonomy level shall occur absent
documented approval under the Company’s governance procedures.

### Section 4.5 Prohibited Drift
No Person acting for the Series shall knowingly use a Covered System:

(a) outside the approved use case boundary;  
(b) to evade applicable law, auditability, or recordkeeping;  
(c) to conceal automated acts from required human review; or  
(d) to defeat any access, monitoring, or trace-preservation control.

## ARTICLE V  
## TRACE CREATION; MINIMIZATION; EXTERNAL SEPARATION

### Section 5.1 General Duty
The Series shall create and preserve Trace Records for each Covered Event in a
manner reasonably sufficient to permit ex post review of the relevant inputs,
observations, actions, approvals, and use.

### Section 5.2 Unit of Preservation
The auditable unit for preservation and review shall ordinarily consist of the linked
bundle of Reasoning Trace (if any), Action Trace, Observation Trace, and associated
metadata, and not Reasoning Trace in isolation.

### Section 5.3 Reasoning Trace Minimization
The Series shall minimize unnecessary creation, retention, exposure, export, and
distribution of Reasoning Trace, consistent with safety, audit, preservation, and
compliance needs.

### Section 5.4 Separation from External Explanation
The Series may provide external explanations, summaries, or justification statements
without disclosing full internal or logged Reasoning Trace. External explanation
artifacts may be separated from internal Trace Records where appropriate to protect
confidentiality, security, and capability integrity.

### Section 5.5 Mandatory Capture Events
Trace Records shall be preserved for, at minimum:

(a) material legal or compliance analyses;  
(b) contract drafting, clause selection, redlining, or negotiation support materially relied upon;  
(c) automated or semi-automated submissions, payments, notices, filings, or updates;  
(d) agentic tool use with external effect;  
(e) anomalous, unsafe, materially incorrect, or policy-violating outputs or actions; and  
(f) any matter designated by counsel, the AI Compliance Manager, or the Evidence Custodian.

## ARTICLE VI  
## INTEGRITY; AUTHENTICITY; PROVENANCE

### Section 6.1 Integrity Controls
The Series shall maintain reasonable technical and organizational safeguards to
preserve the integrity, authenticity, provenance, and reviewability of Trace Records.

### Section 6.2 Minimum Controls
To the extent commercially and technically reasonable, such safeguards shall include:

(a) append-only or WORM-equivalent controls for designated critical records;  
(b) timestamping;  
(c) record-level or batch-level hashing;  
(d) digital signature or equivalent verification for designated critical exports;  
(e) provenance mapping sufficient to link a Covered Event to its Trace Records;  
(f) role-based access control;  
(g) access logging; and  
(h) version control for prompts, policies, and system configurations.

### Section 6.3 Chain of Custody
The Evidence Custodian shall maintain a chain-of-custody protocol and linkage index
sufficient to connect material Covered Events with their associated Trace Records and
their preservation history.

---

## ARTICLE VII  
## RETENTION; LEGAL HOLD

### Section 7.1 Material Trace Retention
Trace Records relating to material Covered Events shall be retained for not less than
ten (10) years after the later of:

(a) the date of the applicable Covered Event; and  
(b) the last material reliance, use, external delivery, or enforcement activity relating
to such Covered Event,

unless a longer period is required by law, contract, or legal hold.

### Section 7.2 Non-Material Operational Logs
Operational logs not designated as material Trace Records shall be retained for not
less than six (6) months, or such longer period as applicable policy requires.

### Section 7.3 Legal Holds
Upon a Legal Hold Event, ordinary deletion, overwriting, or destruction of relevant
Trace Records shall be suspended promptly and preserved until release by authorized
counsel or the Evidence Custodian.

---

## ARTICLE VIII  
## ACCESS CONTROL; SECURITY; ANOMALY DETECTION

### Section 8.1 Restricted Access
Access to Reasoning Trace shall be limited on a strict need-to-know basis. Action
Trace and Observation Trace may be made more broadly available internally where
necessary for audit, legal, operational, or safety purposes, subject to applicable
confidentiality controls.

### Section 8.2 Least Privilege
The Series shall implement least-privilege access controls for all Trace Records and
shall prohibit unnecessary inter-series access, copying, or transfer.

### Section 8.3 Reasoning Trace Export Restriction
No Reasoning Trace shall be externally disclosed, exported, or transferred except:

(a) as required by law or valid legal process;  
(b) as reasonably necessary for audit, investigation, or defense under appropriate
confidentiality protections; or  
(c) with written approval by the Manager and counsel, subject to documented purpose
limitation and disclosure logging.

### Section 8.4 Anomalous Access Monitoring
The Series shall maintain commercially reasonable controls to detect anomalous
access patterns, bulk extraction, unusual query behavior, excessive export activity, or
other signals reasonably associated with model extraction, distillation, or reverse-
engineering risk.

### Section 8.5 Incident Escalation
Any suspected unauthorized extraction, unusual bulk access, large-scale copying,
cross-boundary leakage, or compromise of Trace Records shall be escalated promptly
to the AI Compliance Manager and Evidence Custodian, with documented remedial
action.

---

## ARTICLE IX  
## CROSS-SERIES CALLS; DELEGATION; ERROR PROPAGATION

### Section 9.1 Cross-Series Delegation
Where the Series delegates a task, subtask, tool call, or workflow step to another
series, service unit, or external provider, the delegation boundary shall be
documented.

### Section 9.2 Cross-Series Provenance
The Company and the relevant series shall maintain provenance records sufficient to
reconstruct the sequence of delegation, return flow, and responsibility allocation
across the applicable series or systems.

### Section 9.3 Error Propagation Control
For material cross-series or external delegations, the Series shall implement
reasonable boundary validation, verification, re-review, or re-reasoning controls
where necessary to reduce propagation of erroneous observations, unsafe actions, or
misleading reasoning expressions.

### Section 9.4 Security Boundaries
No cross-series sharing of Reasoning Trace shall occur except where specifically
authorized, logged, and justified by operational necessity, audit need, or legal
requirement.

---

## ARTICLE X  
## AUDIT; REVIEW; REPORTING

### Section 10.1 Internal Audit
The Series shall undergo periodic audit of:

(a) trace creation and preservation;  
(b) Action Trace completeness;  
(c) access control effectiveness;  
(d) anomaly detection and incident handling;  
(e) cross-series provenance controls; and  
(f) human oversight performance.

### Section 10.2 Review Metrics
Where appropriate to the use case, the Series may evaluate Reasoning Trace using
metrics such as faithfulness, reproducibility, causal stability, or other review criteria;
provided, however, that such metrics shall supplement and not displace Action Trace-
based accountability.

### Section 10.3 Reporting
The Manager may require periodic internal reports and, where appropriate to the
governance model, external or regulator-facing reports concerning the Series’
traceability, safety controls, material incidents, and compliance posture.

---

## ARTICLE XI  
## THIRD-PARTY PROVIDERS; FLOW-DOWN OBLIGATIONS

### Section 11.1 Required Terms
Any provider, vendor, contractor, or intermediary materially involved in a Covered
System shall, where commercially feasible, be bound by written terms addressing:

(a) trace and log availability;  
(b) model/version and material change notice;  
(c) incident cooperation;  
(d) export or preservation support on exit;  
(e) confidentiality and security; and  
(f) assistance reasonably necessary to preserve provenance and auditability.

### Section 11.2 Hidden Internal Reasoning
No provider shall be required by this Rider to disclose hidden internal reasoning,
latent states, or proprietary internal model processes not ordinarily exposed by the
provider; provided that any substitute explanation artifact, trace summary, audit
artifact, or provider certification made available shall be preserved where material.

---

## ARTICLE XII  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 12.1 Material Breach
Any knowing destruction, falsification, concealment, disabling, unauthorized export,
or unauthorized alteration of material Trace Records shall constitute a material breach
of the LLC Agreement as applied to the Series.

### Section 12.2 Remedies
The Manager may suspend access, require corrective action, impose governance
restrictions, seek indemnification or contribution, or pursue equitable relief to the
extent permitted by the LLC Agreement and applicable law.

### Section 12.3 Survival
This Rider shall survive dissolution, winding up, transfer, merger, conversion, or
termination of the Series to the extent necessary to enforce preservation,
confidentiality, access-control, audit, reporting, indemnification, and remedial
obligations arising from events occurring prior thereto.

### Section 12.4 Governing Law
This Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to
conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.
