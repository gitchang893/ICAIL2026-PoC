# Common Cbase AI Governance Rider

## Baseline Series-Level Addendum to Limited Liability Company Agreement

This Common Cbase AI Governance Rider (this “Cbase Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, and forms part of, the Limited Liability Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”).

This Cbase Rider sets forth the baseline governance clauses applicable to any series of the Company that is expressly designated by the Company as subject to **Cbase**. Additional series-specific clauses may be adopted by separate addendum, supplement, schedule, or rider.

In the event of any inconsistency between this Cbase Rider and a validly adopted series-specific add-on, the series-specific add-on shall control solely with respect to the applicable series and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that AI-assisted systems may be deployed across multiple use cases and risk tiers, and that each such use case requires, at minimum, baseline rules for purpose limitation, supervisory authority, change control, logging, and accountability;

WHEREAS, the Company intends that this Cbase Rider shall operate as the baseline governance layer for all designated AI-related series, regardless of whether such series is subject to additional high-risk, transparency, or other supplemental controls;

WHEREAS, the Company further intends that legal and organizational responsibility for the use, supervision, and consequences of AI Systems shall remain with the Company, the applicable series, and designated natural persons, and shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System itself;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Cbase Rider as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; APPLICABILITY; INTERPRETATION

### Section 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Cbase Rider is to establish baseline governance rules for AI-related series of the Company, including:

(a) purpose limitation;  
(b) designation of responsible natural persons;  
(c) baseline supervisory authority;  
(d) change control;  
(e) baseline logging and incident escalation; and  
(f) retention of organizational accountability.

### Section 1.2 Applicability

This Cbase Rider shall apply to any series expressly designated by the Company as subject to **Cbase**, whether standing alone or in combination with additional governance clauses.

### Section 1.3 Interpretive Principle

This Cbase Rider shall be interpreted as a baseline governance instrument and shall not be construed to eliminate, narrow, or displace any heightened controls applicable to a particular series by reason of risk tier, use case, law, or separate Company policy.

### Section 1.4 No Independent AI Personhood

Nothing in this Cbase Rider shall be construed as recognizing legal personhood, independent agency in law, consciousness, sentience, or moral status of any AI System. All authority, responsibility, and accountability remain with the Company, the applicable series, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf.

---

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”

“AI System” means any machine learning system, large language model, reasoning model, retrieval-augmented system, predictive model, decision-support tool, agentic workflow, software automation component, or combination thereof, whether developed internally or provided by a third party, that is used by or for an applicable series.

### Section 2.2 “Approved Use Case Boundary”

“Approved Use Case Boundary” means the purpose, functional scope, data scope, tool scope, user scope, communication scope, and autonomy level expressly approved by the Company for the applicable series.

### Section 2.3 “Material Change”

“Material Change” means any change to the AI System, its provider, its model version, its prompt or workflow architecture, its connected tools, its data inputs, its deployment context, or its intended use that could reasonably affect legality, reliability, safety, security, accountability, or governance posture.

### Section 2.4 “Material Incident”

“Material Incident” means any event, malfunction, unauthorized use, repeated anomaly, significant error pattern, significant data integrity issue, unauthorized external effect, or other occurrence reasonably requiring supervisory attention.

## ARTICLE III  
## BASELINE GOVERNANCE CLAUSES (Cbase)

### Section 3.1 Purpose Limitation

Each applicable series shall operate solely within its Approved Use Case Boundary.

### Section 3.2 Human Responsibility

The Company shall designate, for each applicable series, one or more responsible natural persons with authority appropriate to the relevant use case. Such persons shall include, at minimum:

(a) an operations or use-case manager; and  
(b) a compliance, supervisory, or operations liaison.

### Section 3.3 No Delegation of Ultimate Responsibility

The Company, the applicable series, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf shall remain responsible for the governance, deployment, use, supervision, and consequences of the AI System. Final responsibility shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System.

### Section 3.4 Supervisory Authority

Authorized personnel shall retain authority, as appropriate to the use case, to revise, override, suspend, narrow, or terminate the use of the AI System.

### Section 3.5 Change Control

No Material Change shall be implemented without documented review proportionate to the nature of the change.

### Section 3.6 Baseline Logging

Each applicable series shall maintain baseline records sufficient to identify:

(a) the system version or materially relevant configuration in use;  
(b) principal data sources or operational inputs, where appropriate;  
(c) major workflow or deployment changes; and  
(d) Material Incidents and their handling.

### Section 3.7 Incident Escalation

Material Incidents shall be escalated to designated responsible personnel within a reasonable time and handled in a manner proportionate to the nature of the incident.

### Section 3.8 No Unapproved Higher-Risk Migration

No applicable series shall be used for a materially higher-risk use case than that approved for such series without formal reclassification, reassignment, or adoption of additional controls as determined by the Company.

### Section 3.9 Vendor and Provider Baseline

Reliance on a third-party provider shall not, by itself, eliminate or reduce the governance duties of the applicable series within its Approved Use Case Boundary.

### Section 3.10 Baseline Documentation

The Company shall maintain, for each applicable series, a written record identifying:

(a) the approved use case;  
(b) the responsible personnel;  
(c) the general role of the AI System; and  
(d) the rationale for the selected governance tier.

## ARTICLE IV  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 4.1 Material Breach

Any knowing operation outside the Approved Use Case Boundary, knowing concealment of a Material Incident, knowing circumvention of designated supervisory authority, or knowing use of the applicable series for an unapproved higher-risk function shall constitute a material breach of the LLC Agreement as applied to the applicable series.

### Section 4.2 Remedies

Without limiting any other rights or remedies under the LLC Agreement or applicable law, the Company may:

(a) suspend deployment;  
(b) narrow operational scope;  
(c) require remediation;  
(d) require additional review or documentation;  
(e) reassign governance responsibility; or  
(f) transfer the use case to another governance structure.

### Section 4.3 Survival

This Cbase Rider shall survive dissolution, winding up, transfer, merger, conversion, or termination of any applicable series to the extent necessary to enforce accountability, remediation, documentation, and responsibility provisions arising from events occurring prior thereto.

### Section 4.4 Governing Law

This Cbase Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.
