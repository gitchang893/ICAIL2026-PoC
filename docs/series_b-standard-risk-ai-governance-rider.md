# Series B Standard-Risk AI Governance Rider

## Series-Level Addendum to Limited Liability Company Agreement

This Series B Standard-Risk AI Governance Rider (this “Rider”) is adopted as an Exhibit to, 
and forms part of, the Limited Liability Company Agreement of **[MASTER LLC NAME]**, 
a Delaware limited liability company (the “Company”), effective as of **[DATE]** (the “LLC Agreement”), 
and applies solely with respect to **Series B**, a **[protected series / registered series]** of the Company (the “Series B”).

This Rider is intended to govern a **Standard-Risk AI Use Case**, as such term is defined 
in Section 2.2 below and as further described in the Series Schedule, Use Case Register, 
or equivalent internal designation record of the Company.

In the event of any inconsistency between this Rider and the LLC Agreement, 
this Rider shall control solely with respect to Series B and solely to the extent of such inconsistency.

---

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company acknowledges that not all AI-assisted and agentic systems operate in contexts requiring the heightened governance applicable to High-Risk AI Use Cases, and that many business uses of AI serve operational, analytical, drafting, support, or productivity functions without directly determining legal rights, legal obligations, eligibility, or comparable high-impact outcomes;

WHEREAS, the Company further acknowledges that such lower- or standard-risk use cases nevertheless require a defined governance structure in order to preserve purpose limitation, prevent unauthorized operational expansion, maintain appropriate human supervision, and ensure that such systems do not drift into materially higher-risk use;

WHEREAS, the Company has determined that a **series-based organizational structure** may serve as a purpose-bound governance mechanism for specified non-high-risk or standard-risk AI use cases by concentrating operational responsibility, boundary control, change management, and escalation pathways within a defined legal and operational unit;

WHEREAS, Series B is intended to serve as the Company’s dedicated unit for a specified **Standard-Risk AI Use Case**, and not as a substitute for the heightened governance, approval, or control structure required for a High-Risk AI Use Case;

WHEREAS, the Company intends that Series B shall operate subject to defined use-case boundaries, designated supervisory authority, change control, reclassification triggers, and such other governance controls as are appropriate to standard-risk AI operation;

WHEREAS, the Company further intends that responsibility for the governance, deployment, supervision, and external effects of AI Systems used by or for Series B shall remain with the Company, Series B, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf, and shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System itself;

WHEREAS, the Company desires to implement this Rider in order to formalize the foregoing principles in a binding governance instrument applicable to Series B;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Company hereby adopts this Rider as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I  
## PURPOSE; FUNCTION; RISK POSITIONING

### Section 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this Rider is to establish a governance framework for Series B as the dedicated operating and accountability unit for a Standard-Risk AI Use Case, including:

(a) purpose-binding of the Series to defined non-high-risk or standard-risk activities;  
(b) designation of responsible supervisory authority and operational accountability;  
(c) lawful, reviewable, and controlled deployment and operation of the applicable AI System;  
(d) implementation of change management, escalation, and reclassification controls;  
(e) maintenance of organizational, contractual, and technical safeguards proportionate to standard-risk operation; and  
(f) clarification that legal and organizational responsibility remains with human and entity actors, and not with the AI System itself.

### Section 1.2 Functional Role of Series B

Series B shall function as the Company’s designated governance, deployment, and accountability unit for the applicable Standard-Risk AI Use Case. Series B shall not operate as an unbounded general-purpose AI vehicle.

### Section 1.3 Interpretive Principle

This Rider shall be interpreted to preserve the role of Series B as a purpose-bound governance and accountability unit for a Standard-Risk AI Use Case, and not as an instrument for evading reclassification into a higher-risk governance structure when such reclassification is warranted.

### Section 1.4 No Independent AI Personhood

Nothing in this Rider shall be construed as recognizing independent legal personhood, consciousness, sentience, agency in law, or moral status of any AI System. All authority, responsibility, and accountability under this Rider remain with the Company, Series B, and the relevant natural persons acting on their behalf.

## ARTICLE II  
## DEFINITIONS

### Section 2.1 “AI System”

“AI System” means any machine learning system, large language model, reasoning model, retrieval-augmented system, agentic workflow, automated drafting system, decision-support tool, or combination thereof, whether developed internally or provided by a third party, that is used by or for Series B.

### Section 2.2 “Standard-Risk AI Use Case”

“Standard-Risk AI Use Case” means any use case designated by the Company or the Manager as not requiring the heightened governance applicable to a High-Risk AI Use Case, provided that the relevant AI System is not authorized to make final determinations or perform materially rights-affecting functions without the approvals and controls required by the Company.

### Section 2.3 “Approved Use Case Boundary”

“Approved Use Case Boundary” means the purpose, functional scope, user class, data class, tool scope, communication scope, and autonomy level expressly approved for Series B in the applicable Series Schedule, Use Case Register, deployment approval record, or equivalent governance document.

### Section 2.4 “Material Change”

“Material Change” means any change to the AI System, its provider, its model version, its prompt architecture, its retrieval sources, its connected tools, its external interfaces, its degree of autonomy, its user base, its data scope, its deployment context, or its intended use that could reasonably affect the legal, compliance, operational, reputational, or governance posture of Series B.

### Section 2.5 “Supervising Officer”

“Supervising Officer” means the natural person designated under Section 4.1(c) with authority to review, approve, suspend, override, or escalate matters arising under this Rider.

### Section 2.6 “Reclassification Trigger”

“Reclassification Trigger” means any circumstance in which the use, scope, outputs, external effects, or operational dependence of the AI System indicates that the applicable use case may no longer be appropriately governed as a Standard-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 2.7 “Critical Event”

“Critical Event” means any incident, output, action, malfunction, unauthorized access, prohibited drift, compliance failure, external harm event, or other occurrence that could materially impair legality, safety, reliability, fairness, security, or governance adequacy.

## ARTICLE III  
## PURPOSE-BINDING; USE-CASE LIMITATION; NO HIGH-RISK DRIFT

### Section 3.1 Standard-Risk Use Case Orientation

Series B shall be organized and operated solely for the approved Standard-Risk AI Use Case identified by the Company. No materially distinct AI use case shall be added to Series B without formal approval under the LLC Agreement and applicable Company governance procedures.

### Section 3.2 Boundary Restriction

No person acting for or on behalf of Series B shall use the applicable AI System outside the Approved Use Case Boundary.

### Section 3.3 No Silent High-Risk Migration

Series B shall not be used, whether intentionally or by operational drift, to conduct a High-Risk AI Use Case without formal reclassification, approval, and reassignment to the appropriate governance structure designated by the Company.

### Section 3.4 Documented Use Case Record

The Company shall maintain a written purpose record for Series B identifying, at minimum:

(a) the relevant use case;  
(b) the affected internal users, customers, counterparties, or operational functions;  
(c) the class of outputs, recommendations, or actions involved;  
(d) the level of human review required; and  
(e) the rationale for treatment as a Standard-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 3.5 Narrowing Principle

Any ambiguity concerning the scope of Series B’s authorized function shall be resolved in favor of narrower operation pending clarification by the Manager or other designated authority.

---

## ARTICLE IV  
## SUPERVISION; HUMAN AUTHORITY; RESPONSIBILITY

### Section 4.1 Required Appointments

The Manager shall designate for Series B:

(a) an AI Operations Manager;  
(b) a Compliance Liaison;  
(c) a Supervising Officer; and  
(d) an Incident Response Contact.

### Section 4.2 Human Supervisory Requirement

No output, recommendation, draft, classification, routing instruction, or workflow action generated or supported by the AI System shall be treated as self-authorizing. All materially consequential use shall remain subject to review, supervision, or approval by a natural person to the extent required by the Approved Use Case Boundary and Company policy.

### Section 4.3 No Delegation of Ultimate Responsibility

The Company, the Manager, and the relevant natural persons acting for Series B shall remain responsible for the governance, deployment, use, supervision, and external effects of the AI System. Final responsibility shall not be deemed transferred to the AI System.

### Section 4.4 Override and Suspension Authority

The Supervising Officer, the AI Operations Manager, the Manager, and any other person designated by the Company shall have authority to:

(a) narrow the scope of use;  
(b) suspend operation;  
(c) override outputs or workflow actions;  
(d) disable specific tools or integrations;  
(e) require additional review; and  
(f) escalate the matter for reclassification or shutdown.

### Section 4.5 Escalation Duty

Any person acting for Series B who becomes aware of a Critical Event or Reclassification Trigger shall promptly escalate the matter to the Supervising Officer and Incident Response Contact.

---

## ARTICLE V  
## DEPLOYMENT APPROVAL; CHANGE CONTROL; RECLASSIFICATION

### Section 5.1 Initial Deployment Approval

No AI System shall be deployed for Series B until completion of a documented approval process proportionate to the Standard-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 5.2 Minimum Approval Requirements

The approval process shall include, at minimum:

(a) confirmation of the Approved Use Case Boundary;  
(b) identification of the responsible persons designated under Article IV;  
(c) confirmation of applicable legal, contractual, compliance, and policy requirements;  
(d) review of data scope, tool scope, and external interfaces;  
(e) evaluation of reasonably foreseeable misuse, error, overreliance, and drift risks; and  
(f) formal written approval.

### Section 5.3 Material Change Review

No Material Change shall be implemented without documented review and approval proportionate to the change.

### Section 5.4 Reclassification Review

Where a Material Change, Critical Event, or operational evolution suggests that Series B may no longer be appropriately governed as a Standard-Risk AI Use Case, the Company shall conduct a documented reclassification review.

### Section 5.5 Consequences of Reclassification

If the Company determines that the applicable use case should be treated as high-risk or otherwise subject to heightened governance, continued operation shall be suspended, narrowed, or transferred to the governance structure designated by the Company until the required approval and control conditions are satisfied.

### Section 5.6 Restricted Experimental Use

Experimental, sandboxed, or exploratory use involving Series B shall remain subject to this Rider and shall not be used to avoid approval, review, or reclassification requirements.

---

## ARTICLE VI  
## OPERATIONAL, COMPLIANCE, AND BOUNDARY CONTROLS

### Section 6.1 Proportionate Controls

Series B shall maintain organizational, contractual, and technical safeguards proportionate to the nature and impact of the Standard-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 6.2 Minimum Governance Controls

Such controls shall include, where applicable:

(a) purpose limitation;  
(b) role-based supervisory authority;  
(c) access controls;  
(d) change management;  
(e) incident escalation;  
(f) controlled use of external tools and integrations;  
(g) internal guidance for acceptable use; and  
(h) periodic governance review.

### Section 6.3 Prohibited Use

No person acting for Series B shall knowingly use the AI System:

(a) outside the Approved Use Case Boundary;  
(b) in a manner inconsistent with applicable law or binding internal policy;  
(c) as a substitute for required human judgment in a high-impact matter;  
(d) to conceal automated activity from required supervisors or reviewers; or  
(e) after suspension, narrowing, or loss of required approval.

### Section 6.4 No Implied Authorization for High-Risk Use

Nothing in this Rider shall be construed to authorize Series B to perform a High-Risk AI Use Case solely because the AI System is capable of producing outputs relevant to such a use case.

---

## ARTICLE VII  
## VENDOR, PROVIDER, AND THIRD-PARTY FLOW-DOWN

### Section 7.1 Provider Due Diligence

Before Series B relies materially on a third-party provider, vendor, contractor, or intermediary, the Company shall conduct reasonable diligence proportionate to the Standard-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 7.2 Required Contract Terms

Any material provider or vendor agreement supporting Series B shall, where commercially feasible, include terms addressing:

(a) service description and scope limitation;  
(b) material change notice;  
(c) incident notification and cooperation;  
(d) compliance support;  
(e) suspension, exit, or termination support;  
(f) confidentiality and security; and  
(g) preservation of records reasonably necessary for governance and review.

### Section 7.3 No Provider Shielding of Series B Responsibility

Reliance on a third-party provider shall not, by itself, eliminate or reduce Series B’s governance duties for deployment and use within the Approved Use Case Boundary.

### Section 7.4 Tool and Integration Control

No external tool, plugin, retrieval source, or connected service shall be enabled for Series B unless approved through the applicable governance process.

---

## ARTICLE VIII  
## INCIDENTS; DRIFT; REMEDIATION

### Section 8.1 Critical Event Response

Upon identification of a Critical Event, Series B shall take prompt action proportionate to the nature of the event, including investigation, containment, narrowing of use, suspension where appropriate, and remedial action.

### Section 8.2 Mandatory Escalation Categories

At a minimum, escalation shall be required for:

(a) materially inaccurate, misleading, or harmful outputs;  
(b) unauthorized workflow execution or external communication;  
(c) operation outside the Approved Use Case Boundary;  
(d) compromise of required supervision or review;  
(e) unauthorized data or tool access;  
(f) material compliance failures; and  
(g) any Reclassification Trigger.

### Section 8.3 Corrective Measures

Corrective measures may include:

(a) temporary suspension;  
(b) rollback of system changes;  
(c) withdrawal or correction of outputs;  
(d) re-review by qualified personnel;  
(e) narrowing of operational scope;  
(f) additional approval conditions; and  
(g) transfer to a heightened governance structure.

### Section 8.4 No Continued Use After Unresolved Reclassification Trigger

Where a Reclassification Trigger remains unresolved, Series B shall not continue the affected deployment beyond the interim measures authorized by the Supervising Officer or other designated authority.

---

## ARTICLE IX  
## REVIEW; AUDIT; GOVERNANCE REPORTING

### Section 9.1 Periodic Review

Series B shall be subject to periodic review proportionate to the Standard-Risk AI Use Case.

### Section 9.2 Review Scope

Such review may address, as applicable:

(a) use case drift;  
(b) adequacy of supervision;  
(c) compliance with approved boundaries;  
(d) incident handling;  
(e) vendor dependency;  
(f) deployment controls; and  
(g) continued suitability of Series B as the appropriate governance unit.

### Section 9.3 Governance Reporting

The Manager may require internal governance reports, operational risk reports, incident reports, or other management reporting concerning Series B.

### Section 9.4 Escalation to Heightened Governance

Findings from periodic review may support narrowing of scope, additional conditions of operation, reclassification, transfer to another series, or discontinuation of the use case within Series B.

---

## ARTICLE X  
## SEPARATENESS; CONTAINMENT; CROSS-SERIES RESTRICTIONS

### Section 10.1 Series Separateness

Series B shall maintain operational and governance separateness consistent with the LLC Agreement and applicable law.

### Section 10.2 Cross-Series Restriction

No workflow, dataset, approval chain, or governance function associated with Series B shall be shared with another series except as expressly authorized and documented.

### Section 10.3 Delegation Controls

Where a task or function is delegated across series or to external service units, the scope of delegation, responsible persons, and review responsibility shall be documented.

### Section 10.4 Containment Objective

The purpose of separateness under this Article is to reduce spillover of governance failure, operational drift, legal exposure, and control breakdown across unrelated use cases.

### Section 10.5 No Evasion by Internal Reallocation

No transfer, delegation, or internal restructuring shall be used to evade the approval, review, supervision, incident, or reclassification requirements applicable to Series B.

---

## ARTICLE XI  
## BREACH; REMEDIES; SURVIVAL

### Section 11.1 Material Breach

Any knowing use of the AI System outside the Approved Use Case Boundary, any knowing circumvention of required supervision, any unauthorized continuation after suspension or reclassification restriction, or any knowing concealment of a Critical Event or Reclassification Trigger shall constitute a material breach of the LLC Agreement as applied to Series B.

### Section 11.2 Remedies

Without limiting any other rights or remedies under the LLC Agreement or applicable law, the Manager or the Company may:

(a) suspend personnel access;  
(b) revoke deployment authority;  
(c) require remedial measures;  
(d) reassign governance responsibility;  
(e) seek indemnification or contribution where permitted; and  
(f) terminate the affected deployment.

### Section 11.3 Survival

This Rider shall survive dissolution, winding up, transfer, merger, conversion, or termination of Series B to the extent necessary to enforce supervision, remediation, reporting, confidentiality, and responsibility provisions arising from events occurring prior thereto.

### Section 11.4 Governing Law

This Rider shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts-of-law principles, except to the extent mandatory non-waivable law applies.

## SCHEDULE 1  
## AUTHORIZED BUSINESS AND STANDARD-RISK USE CASE DESCRIPTION OF SERIES B

This Schedule 1 is adopted for the limited purpose of identifying the authorized business of Series B and describing the Standard-Risk AI Use Case contemplated by this Rider. This Schedule 1 is intended to aid interpretation and administration of the Rider and shall not, by itself, expand the Approved Use Case Boundary except to the extent expressly approved pursuant to the LLC Agreement and the governance procedures of the Company.

### Section 1.1 Authorized Character of Business

Series B shall be organized and operated solely as a purpose-bound operating and governance unit for a Standard-Risk AI Use Case in which an AI System is used to assist with drafting, summarization, search, internal analysis, workflow support, operational routing, productivity enhancement, customer support preparation, or comparable support functions that do not, within the Approved Use Case Boundary, constitute final high-impact decision-making.

### Section 1.2 Authorized Business Categories

Subject to the Approved Use Case Boundary, the authorized business of Series B may include only the following categories of activity, to the extent specifically approved by the Company:

(a) AI-assisted drafting, summarization, translation, classification, or research support for internal business operations;  

(b) AI-assisted customer service support, including draft response generation, issue routing, prioritization, and internal support workflows, provided that materially consequential external actions remain subject to required human review;  

(c) AI-assisted internal productivity and knowledge-management functions, including document search, internal question-answering, meeting support, and workflow assistance;  

(d) AI-assisted routine operational support functions not designated by the Company as high-risk; and  

(e) such other non-high-risk or standard-risk business activities as may be specifically authorized in writing by the Company and incorporated into the Approved Use Case Boundary.

### Section 1.3 Excluded Activities

Unless expressly approved in writing pursuant to the LLC Agreement and the applicable governance procedures, Series B shall not be used for:

(a) final determinations affecting legal rights, legal obligations, eligibility, regulatory filings, or comparable high-impact matters;  

(b) autonomous external commitments, legally operative communications, or materially significant workflow execution without heightened approval;  

(c) high-risk decision-support activities reserved to another series or governance structure; or  

(d) any activity that would materially expand the scope, affected population, external impact, or autonomy level of the approved Standard-Risk AI Use Case without re-approval and, where applicable, reclassification.

### Section 1.4 Assumed Operational Conditions

For purposes of interpreting this Rider, the Standard-Risk AI Use Case of Series B shall be understood to contemplate one or more of the following operational conditions:

(a) the AI System is used primarily to support human users rather than to replace final human judgment in high-impact matters;  

(b) the AI System may influence drafting, summarization, prioritization, routing, or internal workflow preparation;  

(c) the AI System may be connected to limited tools, repositories, or communication systems subject to the boundaries approved by the Company; and  

(d) misuse, overreliance, or drift may create legal, reputational, contractual, or operational harm even where the use case is not initially classified as high-risk.

### Section 1.5 Governing Characterization

Series B shall be characterized, for all purposes of this Rider, as a dedicated standard-risk operating and accountability unit and not as a means to avoid or dilute the governance requirements applicable to a High-Risk AI Use Case.

## SCHEDULE 2  
## ILLUSTRATIVE RISK EVENTS, INCIDENT CATEGORIES, AND GOVERNANCE FAILURES

This Schedule 2 is adopted for the limited purpose of identifying the types of incidents, failures, disputes, and governance concerns for which this Rider is intended to provide a control structure. The categories listed below are illustrative and non-exclusive. Inclusion in this Schedule 2 shall not be construed to create independent authorization for any activity otherwise prohibited by the Rider.

### Section 2.1 Output-Related Risk Events

Illustrative output-related risk events include:

(a) generation of inaccurate, incomplete, misleading, or unusable drafts, summaries, or classifications;  

(b) fabricated, hallucinatory, or unsupported citations, authorities, facts, or references;  

(c) material misrouting, misclassification, or misprioritization of internal matters or customer-facing issues;  

(d) omission of relevant context, warnings, or escalation indicators; and  

(e) plausible but unreliable outputs that create substantial risk of overreliance by human users.

### Section 2.2 Action-Related Risk Events

Where the AI System is connected to tools, workflows, or external interfaces, illustrative action-related risk events include:

(a) unauthorized draft transmission or communication;  

(b) execution of a workflow step beyond the approved role of the system;  

(c) transmission to an incorrect recipient, system, or repository;  

(d) continued automated operation after narrowing or suspension should have occurred; and  

(e) external effects materially exceeding the standard-risk role approved for Series B.

### Section 2.3 Governance and Oversight Failures

Illustrative governance and oversight failures include:

(a) operation outside the Approved Use Case Boundary;  

(b) failure to maintain adequate supervision or escalation pathways;  

(c) implementation of a Material Change without required review;  

(d) silent expansion of tool scope, autonomy level, or operational reliance;  

(e) inappropriate substitution of AI output for required human judgment; and  

(f) failure to escalate a Reclassification Trigger.

### Section 2.4 Vendor, Provider, and Integration Risks

Illustrative third-party and integration risks include:

(a) undisclosed or inadequately disclosed service changes;  

(b) material degradation in model or provider behavior;  

(c) inadequate provider cooperation during incident review;  

(d) activation of tools, plugins, retrieval sources, or connected services without required approval; and  

(e) uncertainty regarding allocation of responsibility as between Series B and a third-party provider.

### Section 2.5 Data, Confidentiality, and Security Risks

Illustrative data, confidentiality, and security risks include:

(a) unauthorized access to confidential, proprietary, customer, or regulated information;  

(b) disclosure or misuse of information across internal boundaries or to unauthorized recipients;  

(c) prompt injection, unsafe retrieval behavior, or manipulation through connected sources;  

(d) unsafe use of tools, permissions, or integrations; and  

(e) compromise of governance controls through excessive automation or inadequate access restriction.

### Section 2.6 Reclassification and Drift Matters

Illustrative Reclassification Triggers include:

(a) increased operational dependence on the AI System in high-impact matters;  

(b) migration from drafting or support use into decision-support affecting rights, obligations, or eligibility;  

(c) use of Series B workflows for matters more appropriately governed as high-risk;  

(d) repeated incidents demonstrating that the existing control structure is insufficient; and  

(e) expansion of autonomy, external effect, or affected population beyond what was originally approved.

### Section 2.7 Dispute and Enforcement Matters

Without limitation, this Rider contemplates that disputes, enforcement actions, or internal investigations may arise concerning:

(a) whether Series B acted within the Approved Use Case Boundary;  

(b) whether required supervision was performed in substance and not merely in form;  

(c) whether a Material Change should have triggered additional review;  

(d) whether a Reclassification Trigger was identified and handled appropriately;  

(e) whether adequate separateness and containment were maintained; and  

(f) whether ultimate responsibility remained appropriately allocated to the Company, Series B, and relevant natural persons rather than being impermissibly displaced onto the AI System.

### Section 2.8 Interpretive Use of Schedule 2

The incident and risk categories set forth in this Schedule 2 may be used, as appropriate, in connection with:

(a) approval and deployment review;  
(b) change-control review;  
(c) risk assessment;  
(d) incident escalation and response;  
(e) periodic review;  
(f) reclassification analysis; and  
(g) interpretation of the purpose, scope, and control requirements applicable to Series B under this Rider.

## SCHEDULE 3  
## INTERPRETIVE PRINCIPLE

This Rider, including Schedules 1 through 3, shall be interpreted on the premise that the central governance question presented by a Standard-Risk AI Use Case is whether an identifiable organizational unit can constrain purpose, preserve human supervision, prevent unapproved drift into higher-risk activity, respond to incidents, and remain answerable for the external effects of AI-assisted operations. Series B is therefore intended to function as a purpose-bound operating and accountability unit of the Company.
